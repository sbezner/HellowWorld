About Low Gap Shelter

AT Mile: 43.3 from Springer
Elevation: 2,966 ft
Capacity: ~6 hikers
County: White County, GA

Low Gap Shelter sits in a draw accessed via a blue-blazed side trail 190 yards east off the AT. Well-used shelter with plenty of tent sites along the path down to it. Between Hogpen Gap to the south and Unicoi Gap to the north.

No bear cable or food storage box. Hang all food properly — this is active black bear country in Georgia.
Peak season privy warning. During peak NOBO season (late Feb–early May), the privy has been reported overflowing and unusable. Plan accordingly.
Water sources: Primary source is 30–40 yards in front of the shelter. Additional spring on the AT itself approximately 0.3 miles north of the shelter. Both reliable in normal conditions.

Exit Options

CLOSEST EXIT

South ~7 mi → Hogpen/Tesnatee Gap (GA-348)

GA Hwy 348 (Richard B. Russell Scenic Hwy) is the closest road crossing. Hike south back through Wildcat Mountain and Tesnatee Gap. Fewer shuttle services cover this gap and GA-348 is a scenic but low-traffic road — hitchhiking is harder than GA-75. Best option if you're injured or need out fast. Helen is ~12 miles east by road.

RECOMMENDED

North ~10 mi → Unicoi Gap (GA-75)

Hike north over Blue Mountain (4,020 ft summit) — a steep, rocky climb — then pass Blue Mountain Shelter (mile 50.7, elevation 3,889 ft, sleeps 7, has bear cables) before descending 2.3 miles to Unicoi Gap. GA-75 has solid traffic. Helen is 9 miles east; Hiawassee is 11 miles west. Most shuttle services cover this gap. Budget ~5–6 hours.

ON-TRAIL OPTION

South ~11.6 mi → Neel Gap (US-19/129)

Mountain Crossings outfitter is on the trail — no road exit or shuttle needed. Good for emergency gear repair, fuel, and basic food. Limited full resupply compared to Hiawassee or Helen. Longer hike than either road exit.

2024 hiker report: Low Gap to Blue Mountain Shelter took ~5 hrs (7.4 miles, steep rocky climb). Then 2.3 miles to Unicoi Gap in ~1 hr. Trail magic with cold drinks reported at Unicoi Gap.

Where to Resupply

Hiawassee, GA
~11 miles west of Unicoi Gap on GA-75 — Top thru-hiker resupply in North Georgia
Walkable Main Street with everything a hiker needs. Free city shuttle stops at Ingles Supermarket (directly across from Trailful) during peak season. Trailful accepts mail drops: 75 North Main Street, Hiawassee GA 30546 — include your name, phone number, and ETA on the box.
Ingles Supermarket (full grocery) Trailful Outdoor Co. — gear, food, mail drops Post Office Laundromat Pharmacy Restaurants Hotels Local Brewery Free cookout at Town Square 2–4pm (Trailful)
Trailful: (706) 521-HIKE Trailful Website
Helen, GA
~9 miles east of Unicoi Gap on GA-75
Georgia's alpine village — popular tourist town closer by road than Hiawassee. Grocery store, post office, restaurants, and lodging. Unicoi State Park (2 miles NE of Helen) offers camping, cabins, showers, laundry, and a restaurant.
Grocery Store Post Office Restaurants Hotels / Motels Unicoi State Park (showers + laundry + restaurant)
