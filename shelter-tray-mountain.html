Tray Mountain Shelter

Appalachian Trail, Georgia — Mile 56.8 (NOBO) • Lean-to • Elevation ~4,200 ft — Updated 2026

Shelter Overview

AT Mile: 56.8 from Springer
Elevation: ~4,200 ft
Capacity: ~8 hikers
Type: Lean-to
County: Towns/Rabun, GA

Tray Mountain Shelter sits high in the Georgia highlands near the summit of Tray Mountain (4,430 ft) — the second-highest peak on the AT in Georgia after Blood Mountain. At mile 56.8, it's the midpoint of the northern Georgia section and one of the more remote shelters in the state. The views from the open summit just above the shelter are among the best in all of Georgia on clear days, with a 360-degree panorama stretching toward the Great Smoky Mountains to the north and Blood Mountain to the south.

The shelter is a well-maintained lean-to with ample tent sites on the broad ridgeline. The high elevation means colder nights than lower Georgia shelters — temperatures in the low 30s°F are common even in April. Wind can be a significant factor on the exposed ridgeline near the summit.

Very remote location. The nearest paved road access requires either hiking south ~6.1 miles to Unicoi Gap (GA-75) or north ~6.5 miles to an intermediate gap with rough forest road access. Plan to self-rescue if injured — this is not a quick bail-out shelter.
Lightning exposure. The open summit and ridgeline near Tray Mountain are exposed to afternoon thunderstorms, common in summer. Monitor weather and be off the summit by early afternoon.
Water: Spring approximately 200 yards north of the shelter on the AT. Reliable but can be light in late summer drought. Carry water from the last reliable source before the final summit approach.
Views: Walk 0.2 miles south from the shelter to the open summit rocks for a full 360° view. On clear days you can see Blood Mountain to the south (15 miles), the Blue Ridge escarpment, and occasionally the distant peaks of the Smokies to the north.

Getting Supplies

Tray Mountain's remote position makes resupply more of a commitment than most Georgia shelters. Your two road options are Unicoi Gap (south) and Dick's Creek Gap (north), with Hiawassee as the primary town for both.

CONTINUE NORTH — 12.6 mi to US-76

Hiawassee, GA via Dick's Creek Gap (US-76)

The preferred option if you have food to continue — push 12.6 miles north to Dick's Creek Gap (AT mile 69.4), the busiest road crossing in Georgia. US-76 carries strong traffic and hitchhiking is reliable. Hiawassee is 11 miles west: Ingles Supermarket, Trailful Outdoor Co. (gear + mail drops), post office, restaurants. Most shuttle services cover Dick's Creek Gap.

BACKTRACK — 6.1 mi south to GA-75

Unicoi Gap (GA-75) → Helen or Hiawassee

Hike 6.1 miles south back to Unicoi Gap (GA-75). From there, Helen is 9 miles east and Hiawassee is 11 miles west. Hitchhiking GA-75 is moderate — less traffic than US-76 but manageable. Best option if you have an injury or need to exit quickly going south.

ROUGH ACCESS — Forest Road nearby

Addis Gap — USFS Road (very rough)

Some topo maps show a rough forest service road accessing the trail near Addis Gap, a few miles north of Tray Mountain. This road is unmaintained and should not be counted on. High-clearance 4WD only, and road conditions are unreliable. Do not attempt to self-shuttle here without local knowledge.

Shuttle tip: If you're going to Dick's Creek Gap, call Stanimal's (407-435-0294) or Bandits AT Shuttles (828-231-7728) before you leave the shelter so they know your ETA. Cell signal from Tray Mountain summit can reach towers — try from the open rocks above the shelter.
