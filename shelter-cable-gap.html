Cable Gap Shelter

North Carolina  ·  AT Mile ~150.8  ·  Lean-to  ·  Elev. ~2,900 ft

Last Shelter Before Fontana Dam Cable Gap is the final shelter before Fontana Dam Shelter — the famous "Hilton of the AT." From here it's approximately 14 more trail miles to Fontana, passing through the final Cheoah ridgelines. You're almost to the Great Smoky Mountains.

Shelter Overview

AT Mile ~150.8
Elevation ~2,900 ft
Capacity 8
Type Lean-to
County Graham, NC
Water Reliable creek 0.1 mi
Privy Yes
Fee Free

Cable Gap Shelter marks the beginning of the final descent toward Fontana Lake and the end of the Cheoah Mountains. After days of demanding ridge climbing through the Cheeohs, the lower elevation here (2,900 ft) and gentler terrain ahead signal that the brutal middle section of the NOC-to-Fontana stretch is nearly behind you. Many thru-hikers feel a real sense of momentum at Cable Gap — Fontana Dam and the Smokies are within reach.

The lean-to sleeps 8 with tent pads nearby. A reliable creek flows approximately 0.1 miles from the shelter on a short side trail. The surrounding forest is a transition zone between the higher elevation hardwoods of the Cheeohs and the mixed hardwood-pine of the Fontana Lake basin. Wildlife is abundant — deer, wild turkey, and black bear are all regularly spotted in this section.

Cable Gap is a natural staging point for the final push to Fontana Dam Shelter. From here, 14 trail miles separate you from one of the AT's most celebrated shelter complexes — with flush toilets, hot showers, and electrical outlets. Most hikers find that knowledge very motivating. The terrain between Cable Gap and Fontana Dam is more moderate than the Cheoah ridgelines to the north.

Fontana Motivation: From Cable Gap, you're 14 miles from Fontana Dam Shelter — the "Hilton of the AT" with flush toilets, showers, and electrical outlets. If that doesn't get you moving in the morning, nothing will.

Getting Supplies

NORTH (RECOMMENDED)

Fontana Village — 14 miles north

~14 trail miles to Fontana Dam, then 2 road miles to Fontana Village. Fontana Village (300 Woods Rd, Fontana Dam, NC 28733) has a camp store, marina store, and restaurant. Push through to Fontana Dam Shelter tonight or tomorrow, then resupply at Fontana Village. This is the standard plan for hikers in the Cheoah section. Stock is limited at Fontana — load up with Smokies food while you can.

SOUTH (EMERGENCY)

Nantahala Outdoor Center

~16.5 trail miles south. The NOC is a very significant backtrack from Cable Gap. Only practical in emergency. See the NOC page for details. Mail drop: 13077 US-19 W, Bryson City, NC 28713.

Smokies Planning: Once at Fontana Village, stock up for the Great Smoky Mountains National Park. The Smokies have very limited resupply options inside the park. Hikers typically carry 4–6 days of food through the Smokies to Davenport Gap.

Where to Sleep Nearby

Cable Gap Shelter (AT Lean-to) Free
On trail · AT Mile ~150.8 · Open year-round · Capacity 8 + tenting
8-person lean-to at the start of the descent toward Fontana Lake. Reliable creek water nearby. Privy on site. Bear cables present. At 2,900 ft, this is one of the lower and warmer shelter sites since the NOC. Tent pads handle overflow. A good staging point if you want to arrive at Fontana Dam Shelter mid-morning the following day.
Pros
  • Free, no reservation
  • Warmer than the high Cheoah shelters
  • Final shelter before the Fontana "Hilton"
  • Reliable water, privy on site
Cons
  • No cell service or road access
  • Active bear area
  • Mice in shelter
  • Still 14 miles from Fontana Dam
Fontana Dam Shelter ("The Hilton") — AT Mile 164.7 Free
~14 trail miles north · Year-round
The famous "Hilton of the AT" — a large, specially built shelter complex near Fontana Dam with flush toilets, hot showers, and electrical outlets. Capacity 24. The most luxurious free shelter on the AT. One long day's hike north from Cable Gap. See the Fontana Dam Shelter page for full details.
Pros
  • Flush toilets and hot showers — free
  • Electrical outlets for charging
  • Large capacity (24)
  • Gateway to Great Smoky Mountains
Cons
  • 14 demanding miles away
  • Can fill with Smokies permit seekers
Fontana Village Resort — Fontana Dam, NC ~$80–140/night
~14 trail miles north + 2 road miles · Seasonal
Lodge rooms, cabins, restaurant, laundry, and small stores at the Fontana Village resort complex. 2 miles from the Fontana Dam Shelter via Fontana Dam Road. Good option if the shelter is full or you want a private room before the Smokies. Call ahead: (800) 849-2258. Address: 300 Woods Rd, Fontana Dam, NC 28733.
Pros
  • Private rooms available
  • Restaurant and full laundry
  • Good Smokies staging point
Cons
  • 14+ miles away
  • Limited services off-season
  • More expensive than the free shelter
