Brown Fork Gap Shelter

North Carolina  ·  AT Mile ~143.8  ·  Lean-to  ·  Elev. ~3,740 ft

Deep Cheoah Backcountry: Brown Fork Gap is in the heart of the Cheoah Mountain section — one of the most remote stretches between the NOC and Fontana Dam. There is no road access and no cell service. Carry 2+ days of food and be prepared for aggressive elevation change in both directions.

Brown Fork Gap Shelter sits deep in the Cheoah Mountains, a rugged range sandwiched between the Nantahala Gorge and Fontana Lake. This section is widely regarded as one of the most demanding on the entire southern AT — steep, relentless ridgelines with significant elevation gain and loss compressed into short distances. The trail crosses Swim Bald (4,760 ft) just north of the shelter, requiring a tough 1,000-foot climb out of the gap.

The shelter is a solid 8-person NHC lean-to in decent condition, maintained by the Nantahala Hiking Club. Brown Fork Creek flows reliably near the shelter and provides one of the more dependable water sources in the Cheoahs. Filter as always. Tent pads accommodate overflow. The privy is maintained on site. Bears are active throughout this section — proper food storage is mandatory.

Brown Fork Gap is roughly the midpoint between Sassafras Gap (south) and Cable Gap (north) in the Cheoah section, making it a natural overnight for hikers who left the NOC 9+ miles ago and still have 7 more miles to Cable Gap. The terrain is challenging enough that many hikers underestimate how long it takes to cover miles here; plan for slower-than-average progress through the Cheoahs.

Cheoah Pacing: Miles in the Cheoahs take longer than miles elsewhere. Factor in the steep climbs and rocky footing. Don't plan big mileage days in this section without experience on comparable terrain. Swim Bald directly north requires a tough 1,000-foot ascent.

Getting Supplies

No road access in either direction from Brown Fork Gap. Resupply must be planned from the NOC (south) or Fontana Village (north).

SOUTH

Nantahala Outdoor Center

~9.5 trail miles south. The NOC at Wesser is the nearest resupply point — a significant backtrack from Brown Fork Gap. Only practical if you have an emergency. The NOC carries food, fuel, and gear at the Wesser Store. See the NOC page. Mail drop: 13077 US-19 W, Bryson City, NC 28713.

NORTH

Fontana Village

~21 trail miles north. Fontana Village (300 Woods Rd, Fontana Dam, NC 28733) is the next resupply option north of Brown Fork Gap. The Fontana Village camp store and marina store have limited supplies — mainly snacks, some freeze-dried meals, and basic gear. Better than nothing after the Cheoah push but not a full resupply. Plan food for 3 days from here to Fontana.

EMERGENCY

Stecoah Gap (NC-143)

~4 trail miles north on AT. Stecoah Gap is a road crossing with NC-143. Hitchhiking to Robbinsville (~12 road miles west) is sometimes possible for emergency exits. Robbinsville has a grocery store and basic services. Not a routine resupply option, but a known bailout point if weather or injury requires leaving the trail.

Load Up at the NOC: This is the critical planning point. Before leaving the NOC, carry at least 3 full days of food. The Cheoah section has no practical resupply until Fontana Village, 30+ miles from the NOC.
