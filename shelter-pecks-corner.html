Pecks Corner Shelter

Great Smoky Mountains NP  ·  AT Mile ~213.0  ·  Lean-to  ·  Elev. ~5,280 ft

🏔 Great Smoky Mountains National Park — Permit Required Purchase at recreation.gov before entering the park. Cost: $40 + $8/person/night. Must stay at the shelter listed on your permit. Chain-link enclosure gate must stay latched. Call (865) 436-1231 to modify reservations.

Pecks Corner Shelter sits at the junction of the AT and the Hughes Ridge Trail — a junction point known as Pecks Corner on older maps. At 5,280 feet, the shelter is in the high-elevation mixed hardwood and spruce zone that characterizes the eastern Smokies. The terrain here begins to feel more remote than the heavily-trafficked Newfound Gap section; the Hughes Ridge area sees fewer day hikers, and the ridgeline stretches northeast toward the less-visited eastern Smokies.

The shelter is a standard chain-link enclosed GSMNP lean-to sleeping 12. A reliable spring nearby provides water year-round. The privy is maintained on site. The surrounding forest shows the layered character of the Smokies — old-growth hardwoods in the coves and hollows, spruce-fir on the exposed ridges, with a dense understory of rhododendron that is spectacular in June when it blooms across entire hillsides.

This section of the AT between Icewater Spring and Tricorner Knob is the quietest stretch of the Smokies traverse — day hikers rarely venture this far from Newfound Gap, and it has a genuine backcountry feel. Wildlife sightings are common here: white-tailed deer are frequently seen, and black bears in this section are among the most active in the Smokies. Keep food stored properly at all times.

Eastern Smokies Wildlife: The section from Pecks Corner east through Tricorner Knob and Cosby Knob sees fewer people and more wildlife than the western Smokies. Bears, deer, wild turkey, and even elk (reintroduced on the TN side) are frequently seen. Stay alert and give animals distance.

Getting Supplies

RECOMMENDED — WEST

Gatlinburg, TN via Newfound Gap

~9.3 trail miles west to Newfound Gap, then ~15 road miles west. Newfound Gap is the closest road crossing with reliable hitchhiking west to Gatlinburg. From Pecks Corner, this is a significant backtrack of 9+ miles. For NOBO hikers who have already passed Newfound Gap, this means returning west — not ideal. Plan food from Gatlinburg to carry all the way through to Davenport Gap (~14 trail miles east of here) if you can.

ALTERNATE — EAST

Cosby, TN / Newport area

~14 trail miles east to Davenport Gap, then road to Cosby or Newport. From Davenport Gap (TN-32/NC-284), you can hitchhike to Cosby, TN (limited services) or Newport, TN (~20 road miles, full grocery). Most hikers push through to Davenport Gap then continue to Hot Springs, NC rather than diverting to Newport.

Food Plan: If you resupplied in Gatlinburg, you should have enough food to carry from Newfound Gap through Pecks Corner to Davenport Gap (~20 trail miles). Pack 2–3 days from Gatlinburg minimum for the eastern Smokies section.
