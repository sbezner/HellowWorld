Wayah Bald Shelter

North Carolina  ·  AT Mile ~115.4  ·  Lean-to  ·  Elev. ~5,100 ft

Historic CCC Fire Tower on Summit Wayah Bald (5,342 ft) features a restored 1930s Civilian Conservation Corps stone fire tower just 0.1 miles from the AT. The observation deck offers one of the finest 360-degree views in the southern Appalachians. Do not skip this summit.

Wayah Bald Shelter sits just below the summit of Wayah Bald in a sheltered hardwood hollow. The shelter itself is standard Nantahala Hiking Club lean-to construction — 8-person capacity, sleeping platform, three walls, roof — but its location makes it one of the most memorable overnight spots on the southern AT. Just 0.1 miles away, the summit of Wayah Bald (5,342 ft) holds a beautifully restored 1930s stone fire tower built by the Civilian Conservation Corps.

The CCC fire tower's observation deck at the top provides unobstructed 360-degree views across the Nantahala National Forest. On clear days you can see the Great Smoky Mountains to the north, the Nantahala Gorge cutting through the ridges below, and the high balds stretching south toward Standing Indian. Sunsets from this tower are legendary among AT thru-hikers. The tower is open to the public and climbers (no lock), but inspect the structure before climbing — verify handrails are secure.

A reliable spring on a signed blue-blaze side trail provides water. The spring is well-maintained and flows through most of the year, though in extreme drought conditions late summer hikers may find reduced flow. Always filter. Tent pads near the shelter accommodate overflow; the area around Wayah Bald is popular with day hikers arriving via a paved USFS access road on the summit, which means this shelter sees more foot traffic than its remote feel suggests.

Day Hiker Traffic: Wayah Bald is accessible by car via USFS Road 69 from the south. Expect day hikers and tourists on weekends. The summit area can be crowded on fall weekends — the shelter sees more visitors than the isolated Nantahala location implies.
The Fire Tower: Climb to the observation deck for sunrise or sunset if you stay at this shelter. The Cherokee called this area "Wayah" meaning wolf — and the summit still feels wild despite the road access below.

Getting Supplies

NORTHBOUND

Nantahala Outdoor Center (NOC) — Wesser, NC

~18.5 trail miles north. The NOC is the primary resupply north of Franklin. After Wayah Bald, the trail passes Wesser Bald Shelter (~mile 131.2) before descending through Rufus Morgan Shelter to arrive at the Nantahala River at the NOC. The outfitter store carries food, fuel, and gear; River's End Restaurant serves hot meals. Plan 2 days of food from Wayah Bald to the NOC. See the NOC page for full details.

SOUTHBOUND

Franklin, NC via Winding Stair Gap

~13.5 trail miles south to gap, then ~10 road miles west. For hikers who skipped Franklin or need to resupply before the long NOC stretch, heading back south to Winding Stair Gap is the only road option. This is a significant backtrack for NOBO hikers — plan ahead from Franklin to avoid it.

Planning Note: Leave Franklin or the NOC with enough food for 2 full days. There is no road access from Wayah Bald itself — the USFS access road on the summit leads to private land areas, not trail towns.
