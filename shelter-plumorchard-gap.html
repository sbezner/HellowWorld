Plumorchard Gap Shelter

Appalachian Trail, Georgia — Mile 68.4 (NOBO) • Lean-to • Elevation ~4,080 ft — Last shelter before North Carolina

Dick's Creek Gap (US-76) is just 1 mile north — and North Carolina begins ~9 miles ahead at Bly Gap. This is the final shelter in Georgia. After crossing into NC, the next shelter is Muskrat Creek Shelter in the Nantahala National Forest.

Shelter Overview

AT Mile: 68.4 from Springer
Elevation: ~4,080 ft
Capacity: ~8 hikers
Type: Lean-to
Next State: NC border ~9 mi north
County: Rabun, GA

Plumorchard Gap Shelter is the last shelter in Georgia and one of the most strategically positioned on the entire southern AT. Sitting just 1 mile south of Dick's Creek Gap (US-76) — the busiest highway crossing in Georgia — it offers superb access to Hiawassee for a final Georgia town stop before crossing into North Carolina.

Most NOBO thru-hikers use this shelter as a pre-NC staging point. You can exit to Hiawassee in the morning, resupply, and return to the trail to push into North Carolina the same day or the next. The shelter sits at a moderately high elevation with access via a short blue-blazed side trail off the AT. Tent sites are available in the surrounding area.

Beyond Dick's Creek Gap, the trail climbs through the final Georgia highlands to Bly Gap (~mile 78) — the actual Georgia/North Carolina state line. The crossing is marked with a simple sign and a change in the character of the forest, with Fraser fir and spruce appearing as you climb into the higher Nantahala terrain.

NC requires different planning. North Carolina shelters and towns require new logistics. Stock up in Hiawassee — the next town access after Dick's Creek Gap is Fontana Dam or Bryson City, NC, both significantly further. Carry adequate food for the next 25–30 miles into NC before your next resupply.
Water: Spring accessible via a short blue-blazed side trail near the shelter. Reliable in most conditions. Also check the creek at the bottom of the approach from Dick's Creek Gap.
Georgia milestone: At mile 68.4, you have hiked almost the full length of the Georgia AT (approximately 75 miles total to the NC border). Congratulations — most thru-hiker attrition happens in Georgia. If you're still going, you're doing it.

Getting Supplies

RECOMMENDED — 1 mi north to US-76

Hiawassee, GA via Dick's Creek Gap (US-76)

Hike just 1 mile north to Dick's Creek Gap — the easiest road exit on the Georgia AT relative to a nearby shelter. US-76 westbound to Hiawassee (11 miles) carries heavy traffic; average hitch wait is 10–20 minutes. Ingles Supermarket has the best grocery selection. Trailful Outdoor Co. handles gear, mail drops, and a free cookout at Town Square 2–4pm. Post office, pharmacy, and laundromat all in Hiawassee. Free city shuttle runs during peak season. See the full Hiawassee guide.

ALTERNATE — 1 mi north + 13 mi east

Clayton, GA via Dick's Creek Gap (US-76 East)

From Dick's Creek Gap, head east 13 miles on US-76 to Clayton. Growing mountain town with Ingles grocery, Sunburst Trails outfitter, breweries, and good restaurants. Less hiker-infrastructure than Hiawassee but a more vibrant food scene. Hitchhiking east from Dick's Creek is slower. Uber/Lyft sometimes available.

LAST CHANCE IN GA

Stock Up Before Crossing Into NC

After Dick's Creek Gap, the next major resupply opportunity is in North Carolina — either Franklin, NC (~30 trail miles from the border) or Fontana Dam. Carry enough food for at least 3–4 days when you leave Dick's Creek Gap heading north. Hiawassee is your last chance for a full Georgia resupply.

Trailful Outdoor Co. mail drop address: 75 North Main St, Hiawassee GA 30546. Hold for AT Hiker. Include name, phone, and ETA. Call (706) 521-HIKE to arrange.
