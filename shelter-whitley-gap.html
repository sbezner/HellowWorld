Shelter Overview

AT Mile: 36.5 from Springer
Elevation: ~3,500 ft
Capacity: ~6 hikers
Type: Lean-to
County: White/Union, GA

Whitley Gap Shelter sits on a ridge between Neel Gap (mile 31.7) and Low Gap (mile 43.3). Located roughly 4.8 miles north of Neel Gap, this shelter is a natural stopping point for hikers who spent the previous night at Blood Mountain or Neel Gap and are pushing toward Hogpen Gap and Low Gap.

The trail in this section traverses the ridgeline east of the Chattahoochee National Forest boundary, passing through rhododendron tunnels and mixed hardwood forests. Whitley Gap sees far less traffic than Blood Mountain or Low Gap, offering a quieter overnight experience. The shelter is accessed via a short blue-blazed side trail from the AT.

Verify condition before arriving. Whitley Gap is a lower-visited shelter and some hiker reports have noted it needs maintenance. Check recent trail reports on FarOut (Guthook) or WhiteBlaze before counting on a night here in bad weather.
Water: Spring source is accessible via a short blue-blazed path from the shelter. Treat all water. Reliable in normal conditions but can be marginal in late summer drought.

Getting Supplies

The nearest road access from Whitley Gap is Hogpen Gap / Tesnatee Gap (GA-348 / Richard B. Russell Scenic Highway), approximately 6.8 miles north on the AT. This highway runs between Blairsville (west) and Helen (east) but is a low-traffic scenic road.

RECOMMENDED — 4.8 mi back south

Mountain Crossings at Neel Gap — Mile 31.7

If you need supplies urgently, backtrack 4.8 miles south to Mountain Crossings at Neel Gap. The AT literally passes through the building — best selection of hiker food, fuel, and gear repair in the area. Or continue north 6.8 miles to Hogpen Gap and hitch to Helen or Blairsville.

NORTH — 6.8 mi ahead to road

Helen, GA via Hogpen/Tesnatee Gap (GA-348)

Hike 6.8 miles north to Hogpen Gap (GA-348). Turn right (east) toward Helen (~12 miles). Helen is a quirky Bavarian-themed tourist town with grocery stores, restaurants, hotels, and basic supplies. Hitchhiking GA-348 is harder than US-76 or GA-75 — it's a scenic byway with lower traffic. Unicoi State Park is 2 miles northeast of Helen with camping and showers.

SERVICES — 6.8 mi north + 20 mi west

Blairsville, GA via GA-348 West

From Hogpen Gap, turn left (west) on GA-348 toward Blairsville (~20 miles by road). Full grocery, pharmacy, and chain hotels. Much farther than Helen but has better supply options (Ingles supermarket). Only worth it for a serious resupply or gear issue.

Hotels in Helen, GA
~$80–200/night
Helen, GA — ~12 miles east of Hogpen Gap on GA-348 (reach via Hogpen Gap hitch)
Helen is a heavily Bavarian-themed tourist town with dozens of hotels and B&Bs. Prices vary widely — budget options exist but the town caters heavily to tourists. Grocery store (Ingles), post office, restaurants including breweries. More of a weekend tourist feel than hiker-focused, but functional as a resupply stop.
