Mountaineer Falls Shelter was built in 2006 by the Tennessee Eastman Hiking and Canoeing Club (TEHCC), making it one of the newest shelters maintained by the club. The wood A-frame design includes a sleeping loft for extra capacity, accommodating up to 14 hikers between the main level and loft. The shelter is located 70 yards off the AT on a blue-blazed trail in Carter County, Tennessee.

The shelter features multiple fire pits, a wash pit, a bar table, and the sleeping loft that provides additional covered space. The water source is Mountaineer Falls branch, located a few hundred yards down a blue-blazed trail — a good, reliable source. The surrounding area offers decent tent sites for overflow camping.

Be aware of the significant gap to the south: the next shelters in the Stan Murray/Overmountain area are approximately 18 miles away, making this one of the longest shelter-less stretches in this section of the AT. Plan your water, food, and daylight accordingly. Heading north, the trail continues through Carter County toward Dennis Cove Road and eventually Hampton, Tennessee. The shelter is maintained by TEHCC.

Newest TEHCC Shelter: Built in 2006, Mountaineer Falls Shelter showcases modern AT shelter design with its A-frame construction and sleeping loft. The loft is a great option when the main level fills up. The wash pit is a nice amenity — use it for cleaning cookware rather than washing in the stream.

Getting Supplies

Mountaineer Falls is between two road crossings that provide access to trail towns. Neither is particularly close, so plan your food carries accordingly.

BEHIND YOU

US 19E — Roan Mountain/Elk Park, ~11 mi south

~11 trail miles south to US 19E. From US 19E you can hitch into Roan Mountain or Elk Park, Tennessee. Roan Mountain has a Dollar General, several restaurants, and basic supplies. This is likely where you last resupplied if hiking NOBO.

AHEAD

Dennis Cove Rd — Kincora Hostel/Hampton, ~14 mi north

~14 trail miles north to Dennis Cove Rd. Kincora Hostel, run by the legendary Bob Peoples, is located on Dennis Cove Road. From there you can get to Hampton, Tennessee for grocery and restaurant resupply. Kincora also offers a small hiker resupply.
