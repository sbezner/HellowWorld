Icewater Spring Shelter

Great Smoky Mountains NP  ·  AT Mile ~207.3  ·  Lean-to  ·  Elev. ~5,820 ft

Gateway to Charlies Bunion Charlies Bunion — one of the most dramatic rocky outcrops on the entire AT — is just 1 mile north of this shelter. The exposed rocky summit offers cliff-edge views deep into the Smokies. Do not miss it.
🏔 Great Smoky Mountains National Park — Permit Required Purchase at recreation.gov before entering the park. Cost: $40 + $8/person/night. Must stay at the shelter listed on your permit. Gate on bear enclosure must stay latched at all times. Call (865) 436-1231 to modify reservations.

Shelter Overview

AT Mile ~207.3
Elevation ~5,820 ft
Capacity 12
Type Lean-to (chain-link enclosure)
State Line NC / TN border
Water Icewater Spring (cold and reliable)
Privy Yes
Fee Free (permit required)

Icewater Spring Shelter takes its name from the extraordinary spring at its base — the water emerges from the mountain at near-freezing temperature year-round, earning its name through generations of hikers who have cupped their hands into it on hot summer days. The spring is one of the most reliable and voluminous in the Smokies, flowing consistently even in drought years. Filtered, it is exceptional drinking water.

The shelter sits on the high ridge just east of Newfound Gap, at nearly 5,900 feet. This is still high-elevation spruce-fir forest territory — red spruce and Fraser fir dominate, with the characteristic mossy, damp understory of the Smokies' high ridges. The forest here shows significant damage from the woolly adelgid, an invasive insect that has killed a large proportion of the Smokies' Fraser firs; the silver standing snags are part of the landscape's identity now, as the park works on adelgid-resistant fir restoration.

The AT between Newfound Gap and Charlies Bunion is one of the most-traveled sections of trail in the entire United States — day hikers from the Newfound Gap parking area pour onto this trail on every clear weekend day. Expect company. Charlies Bunion, 1 mile north of the shelter, is a rocky outcrop with sheer faces and vertiginous views — one of the signature experiences of the Smokies.

Charlies Bunion: 1 mile north on the AT. Rocky, exposed, and unforgettable. The views from the bunion's edge are among the most dramatic on the southern AT. Go in the morning before the day hikers arrive from Newfound Gap if you want solitude.

Getting Supplies

RECOMMENDED

Gatlinburg, TN via Newfound Gap

~3.4 trail miles west to Newfound Gap (US-441), then ~15 road miles west. Newfound Gap is only 3.4 miles west of Icewater Spring — less than 2 hours of hiking. From the busy gap parking area, hitchhiking to Gatlinburg is very reliable — day hikers driving back to town are the typical rides. Average wait: 10–20 minutes. Gatlinburg has Food City grocery, outfitters, laundry, and a wide range of lodging. This is the best mid-Smokies resupply stop.

MAIL DROP

Gatlinburg, TN General Delivery

General Delivery, Gatlinburg, TN 37738. Many thru-hikers pre-mail a resupply box to Gatlinburg for the post-Newfound Gap section. The post office holds General Delivery packages for 30 days. Coordinate your arrival timing carefully. This covers the eastern Smokies (Icewater Spring through Davenport Gap) and the approach to Hot Springs, NC.

Newfound Gap Cell Signal: The Newfound Gap parking area (US-441) has reliable cell signal — the best in the Smokies. Make your calls, update your weather app, and contact anyone who needs to know your location from here.

Where to Sleep

Icewater Spring Shelter (GSMNP Lean-to) Free (permit req.)
On trail · AT Mile ~207.3 · Permit required · Capacity 12
High-elevation chain-link enclosed lean-to with the best-named spring on the AT. Cold water year-round at the shelter. Privy on site. Chain-link bear enclosure — gate must stay latched. At 5,820 ft, temperatures are cold at night even in summer. One of the more popular Smokies shelters due to proximity to Newfound Gap and Charlies Bunion — reserve early for spring dates.
Pros
  • Exceptional cold spring on-site
  • 1 mile from Charlies Bunion
  • 3.4 miles from Newfound Gap road
  • Beautiful spruce-fir forest setting
Cons
  • Very popular — books fast in spring
  • Day hiker traffic on nearby trail
  • Cold at 5,820 ft
  • Permit required and must be specific
Gatlinburg, TN — Hotels / Hostels ~$30–150/night
3.4 trail miles west + 15 road miles · Year-round
Gatlinburg ranges from budget hiker bunks to resort hotels. Hostel in the Hollow offers bunk beds around $30/night and is hiker-friendly. The Gatlinburg Inn is a historic downtown hotel ($80–120). Chain motels on US-441 run $80–150. Easy hitch from Newfound Gap makes this accessible for a zero day mid-Smokies.
Pros
  • Close road access (3.4 mi + hitch)
  • Full resupply options in town
  • Range of price points
  • Good for a mid-Smokies zero day
Cons
  • Very touristy town atmosphere
  • Prices spike on fall color weekends
  • Need to return to exact shelter on permit
