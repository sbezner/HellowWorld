Springer Mountain Shelter

Appalachian Trail, Georgia — Mile 0 (Southern Terminus) • Lean-to — Southern gateway to the AT

You're at the beginning of the Appalachian Trail. Springer Mountain (3,782 ft) is the southern terminus. The AT white blaze starts at the summit plaque. The shelter is ~0.3 miles from the summit on the approach from USFS Road 42. Most hikers reach Springer via the 8.8-mile Approach Trail from Amicalola Falls State Park.

Shelter Overview

AT Mile: 0 (NOBO)
Elevation: 3,782 ft
Capacity: ~8 hikers
Type: Lean-to (open front)
County: Fannin/Gilmer, GA
Water: Spring 0.2 mi south

Springer Mountain Shelter sits at the southern terminus of the Appalachian Trail. There are actually two shelters on Springer: the main lean-to near the summit approach and an older lower shelter. The main lean-to sleeps ~8 and has a picnic table and privy. Very heavily used during NOBO season (late February–April) as hikers begin their journey to Katahdin.

No on-site water. Water source is a spring ~0.2 miles south on a blue-blazed trail. Treat all water before drinking.
Extremely crowded February–April. During peak NOBO season the shelter and surrounding tent sites fill by mid-afternoon. Arrive early or plan to tent on flat areas nearby.
Springer summit plaque: The AT southern terminus plaque is on the summit rocks ~0.3 miles from the shelter. Hikers traditionally take a photo here before setting out. Cell service is very limited — Verizon has best coverage on the summit.

Getting Supplies

Springer Mountain is deep in the Chattahoochee National Forest. There are no stores within easy walking distance. Most hikers carry food from Amicalola Falls or pre-stage resupply at Mountain Crossings at Neel Gap (32 miles north). Your first real resupply will likely be Neel Gap (mile 31.7) or Dahlonega.

BEST FIRST RESUPPLY

Mountain Crossings at Neel Gap — 31.7 trail miles north

The legendary outfitter at Neel Gap (mile 31.7) is the de facto first resupply for most NOBO hikers. The AT passes directly through the building — no road crossing needed. Carry 4–5 days of food from Amicalola. Mountain Crossings offers gear, food, fuel, pack shakedowns, and a bunk room. ~$25–30/night bunk. 706-745-6095.

PRE-HIKE SUPPLIES

Dahlonega, GA — ~22 miles by road

The nearest full-service town for pre-hike provisioning. Walmart Supercenter, outfitters, restaurants, and lodging. Reached via Forest Service roads to GA-52, then south. Most hikers drive through Dahlonega on the way to Amicalola Falls. Stock up here before your hike begins.

EMERGENCY ONLY

Amicalola Falls State Park Store — 8.8 mi south via Approach Trail

The park store at Amicalola Falls sells basic snacks, drinks, and hiker supplies. It is 8.8 miles back south via the blue-blazed Approach Trail (or ~20 miles by car via USFS roads). Only worth it as an emergency bailout — going back to Amicalola defeats your northbound progress.

Mail drops: Mountain Crossings at Neel Gap accepts resupply boxes. Address: Mountain Crossings, 12471 Gainesville Hwy (US-19 N), Blairsville, GA 30512 — include your name, "HOLD FOR AT HIKER," and approximate arrival date.

Where to Sleep Nearby

Amicalola Falls State Park Lodge
~$100–180/night
Amicalola Falls State Park, Dawsonville, GA — 8.8 mi south via Approach Trail / ~20 mi by car
The classic pre-hike or first/last-night stay for AT hikers. The park lodge sits at the base of the Approach Trail. Amenities include a restaurant, gift shop, showers, and laundry. A hostel bunk room is also available at the trailhead for a lower price. Many hikers spend the night here, register at the park, and begin the Approach Trail the next morning.
Pros
  • Iconic AT starting point
  • Restaurant on site
  • Hiker registration & welcome center
  • Shuttle service available
Cons
  • Expensive for park lodge rooms
  • 8.8 miles from Springer (via trail)
  • Pricey restaurant
Call 706-265-7818 gastateparks.org
Hiker Hostel
~$30–40 bunk / ~$75 private
7693 Hiker Hostel Way, Suches, GA 30572 — ~13 mi by road (near Woody Gap on GA-60)
A beloved hiker hostel in the Suches community, roughly 13 road miles from the trail. Offers shuttles to/from Springer Mountain, Amicalola Falls, and Neel Gap. Owned by the same family for years — genuine hiker community feel. Free camping on grounds for those who prefer to tent.
Pros
  • Shuttle to/from Springer available
  • Genuine hiker community
  • Free tent camping option
  • Cheaper than park lodge
Cons
  • ~13 miles from trail by road
  • Small — book ahead in season
  • Limited food on site
Call 706-945-2876 hikerhostel.com
Dahlonega Hotels (Various)
~$80–150/night
Dahlonega, GA — ~22 miles by road from Springer trailhead
Dahlonega (Georgia's gold rush town) has multiple hotels, B&Bs, and motels suitable for pre-hike staging. Best options include the Barefoot Hills Hotel (hiker-friendly, gear room) and several chain motels on US-19. Full grocery, Walmart, restaurants, and gear shops (including Hiker Yearning) all in town.
Pros
  • Full grocery & Walmart nearby
  • Many restaurant options
  • Good for pre-hike resupply
  • Multiple price ranges
Cons
  • ~22 miles from Springer by road
  • Requires car or shuttle to trailhead
