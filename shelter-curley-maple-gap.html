Curley Maple Gap Shelter sits at 3,070 feet in Unicoi County, Tennessee, along a wooded gap in Cherokee National Forest. Originally built by the USFS in 1961 as a basic concrete block structure, the shelter was heavily remodeled in 2010 with funding from an LL Bean grant. The renovation modernized the shelter while preserving its sturdy concrete block construction, and added a covered eating area — a welcome feature in rainy weather that most AT shelters lack.

The shelter sleeps 14 on wooden platforms, making it one of the larger shelters in this section. The water source is a spring located approximately 100 feet south on the AT — reliable in most conditions. There is no privy at this shelter and no bear cables, so you will need to hang your food bag properly using the surrounding trees. The Tennessee Eastman Hiking and Canoeing Club (TEHCC) maintains this shelter and the surrounding trail section.

One of the distinctive features of Curley Maple Gap is that you can hear freight trains from the shelter, echoing up from the Nolichucky River gorge below. The sound carries remarkably well at night — some hikers find it comforting, others less so. The shelter area has decent tent sites in the gap, and the surrounding hardwood forest provides good tree cover for hammock campers.

TEHCC Maintained: This section of the AT is maintained by the Tennessee Eastman Hiking and Canoeing Club, one of the most active trail maintaining clubs in the southern Appalachians. The 2010 remodel with LL Bean grant funding brought this shelter up to modern standards. Please pack out all trash and practice Leave No Trace to help keep this shelter in good condition.

Getting Supplies

Erwin, Tennessee is the nearest trail town and the primary resupply point for this section. Access is via the Nolichucky River crossing south of the shelter, though the Hurricane Helene bridge destruction requires a road walk detour as of 2026.

NEAREST TOWN

Erwin, TN — ~5 mi south via Nolichucky River

~5 trail miles south via the Nolichucky River crossing (road detour in effect). Erwin is a full-service trail town with a Food Lion grocery store, multiple restaurants, a Dollar General, post office for mail drops, and several outfitters. Uncle Johnny's Nolichucky Hostel & Outfitter is located near the river crossing and offers shuttles into town, resupply, gear, laundry, and bunk beds. Due to the Chestoa Bridge destruction from Hurricane Helene (2024), hikers must follow a 3.6-mile road walk detour to cross the Nolichucky. Full services available in Erwin including pharmacies, hardware store, and medical clinic.
