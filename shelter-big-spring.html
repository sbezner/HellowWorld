Big Spring Shelter

North Carolina  ·  AT Mile ~96.8  ·  Lean-to  ·  Elev. ~4,750 ft

Shelter Overview

AT Mile ~96.8
Elevation ~4,750 ft
Capacity 6
Type Lean-to
County Macon, NC
Water Big Spring (reliable, steps away)
Privy Yes
Fee Free

Big Spring Shelter earns its name — the water source here is one of the most reliable and voluminous on the southern AT. A true spring emerges just steps from the shelter, running cold and clear year-round. This makes Big Spring an appealing water carry stop even for day hikers and section backpackers who aren't staying overnight. The lean-to itself is compact (6 capacity), so tent pads nearby are frequently used when the shelter fills.

At nearly 4,800 feet, the shelter sits in a high-elevation mixed hardwood and red spruce zone. The forest here feels distinctly northern compared to Georgia's oaks and rhododendron — on cold, wet days it can feel almost boreal. Spring arrives late at this elevation; snow is possible through April and even into early May in cold years.

Big Spring sits roughly 4.7 miles north of Carter Gap and about 4.5 miles south of Rock Gap (Winding Stair Gap) — putting it squarely in the middle of the Franklin approach. Many hikers use it as a quick lunch or water stop rather than overnight, but it's a solid shelter option if you arrive late. The proximity to both Carter Gap and Rock Gap means this stretch sees steady traffic from both thru-hikers and weekenders from Franklin.

Water Note: The spring at Big Spring is exceptional — one of the most reliable sources in this section. Fill up here even if you're just passing through, especially before the climb to Siler Bald.

Getting Supplies

RECOMMENDED

Franklin, NC via Rock Gap / Winding Stair Gap (US-64)

~4.5 trail miles north to gap, then ~10 road miles west. Rock Gap (also called Winding Stair Gap) is a very short hike north from Big Spring. From the gap, US-64 heads west ~10 miles to Franklin. Hitchhiking is reliable here. Budget Inn offers a free shuttle from the gap — call ahead. Franklin has Ingles Supermarket, Outdoor 76 outfitter, multiple restaurants, and laundry facilities.

Mail Drop: Outdoor 76, 74 E Main St, Franklin, NC 28734. No hold fee for thru-hikers. One of the best outfitter resupply options in the southern AT.

On trail · AT Mile ~96.8 · Open year-round · Capacity 6 + tenting
Compact lean-to with one of the best water sources on the southern AT steps away. Privy on site. Bear cables or box present — use them. At 4,750 ft, temperatures drop significantly at night even in summer. The shelter fills quickly when it does fill; tent pads are a comfortable backup. Mice are present as at nearly all AT shelters.
