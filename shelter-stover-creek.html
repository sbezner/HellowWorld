Shelter Overview

AT Mile: 2.6 from Springer
Elevation: ~2,900 ft
Capacity: ~8 hikers
Type: Lean-to (open front)
County: Fannin, GA
Water: Stover Creek, year-round

Stover Creek Shelter is the first overnight shelter for NOBO thru-hikers, just 2.6 miles from Springer Mountain. It sits in a pleasant hollow near Stover Creek, offering a reliable creek water source and good tent sites nearby. Most early-season hikers stop here after their first day from Amicalola Falls (via the approach trail, total ~11 miles), making it one of the most crowded shelters in Georgia during peak NOBO season.

The shelter is a standard three-sided lean-to with a sleeping platform, privy nearby, and a picnic table. Short-distance hikers from Springer often use it as a turnaround camp. Expect to share with many other freshly-minted thru-hikers in late February through April.

Bears are active in this area. Use the bear cables (if available) or hang your food properly. Do not leave food unattended at the picnic table.
Water: Stover Creek runs year-round and is the primary water source. The creek crossing is just below the shelter. Filter or treat all water.

Getting Supplies

At mile 2.6, you are still very deep in the national forest. There are no nearby stores. Your realistic supply options are either going back to Amicalola (not recommended unless emergency) or continuing north to Neel Gap at mile 31.7 — the standard first resupply for Georgia NOBO hikers.

STANDARD RESUPPLY — 29 trail miles ahead

Mountain Crossings at Neel Gap — Mile 31.7

The AT passes directly through the Mountain Crossings outfitter building at Neel Gap. This is the universally accepted first resupply for Georgia thru-hikers. Carry enough food from your start point (Amicalola or Dahlonega) to reach here. The store offers food, fuel, gear, and pack shakedowns. Bunk room available.

PRE-HIKE ONLY

Dahlonega, GA — ~22 miles by road

Dahlonega is the main staging town for Springer-area hikers. Walmart, grocery stores, restaurants, and outfitters. Not accessible from the trail without a long road walk or shuttle — plan your food here before starting your hike. You should already have Neel Gap supplies when you reach Stover Creek.

Where to Sleep Nearby

Stover Creek is very remote. The most practical lodging options require either a long shuttle back to Amicalola/Dahlonega or continuing ahead to the Neel Gap area.

Amicalola Falls State Park — Hiker Hostel / Lodge
Bunk ~$30 / Lodge ~$120+
Amicalola Falls State Park — 8.8 mi back via Approach Trail or ~20 mi by road
If you need to bail out early, Amicalola Falls is the closest facility. The park lodge and a hiker hostel room are available. A shuttle back to your car at Amicalola or ahead to Neel Gap can be arranged through the park or local services.
Pros
  • Legitimate bail-out option
  • Restaurant and showers
  • Shuttle options available
Cons
  • Going backwards on the trail
  • Long road shuttle distance
  • Lodge rooms expensive

Hiker Hostel (Suches)
~$30–40 bunk / ~$75 private
7693 Hiker Hostel Way, Suches, GA — ~15 mi by road (shuttle required)
The Hiker Hostel in Suches offers shuttle service from trail access points including Three Forks (near mile 3.5, accessible via USFS Road 58). Call ahead — they are very hiker-friendly and can arrange pickup for early-trip issues.
Pros
  • Shuttle from Three Forks available
  • True hiker hostel culture
  • Reasonably priced
Cons
