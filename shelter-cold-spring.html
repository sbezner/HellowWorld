Shelter Overview

AT Mile ~113.0
Elevation ~4,940 ft
Capacity 6
Type Lean-to
County Macon, NC
Water Cold Spring (namesake, reliable)
Privy Yes
Fee Free

Cold Spring Shelter takes its name from the exceptional spring that flows just steps from the lean-to — one of the coldest and most reliable water sources on the southern AT. Even in late summer drought years, the spring continues to flow, fed by the deep bedrock aquifer of the Nantahala highlands. The water is clear, cold, and inviting; always filter or treat before drinking.

Sitting at nearly 5,000 feet in a mature hardwood forest, this is prime black bear country in the Nantahala National Forest. Bear encounters are common in this section — proper food storage using bear boxes or hang cables is mandatory, not optional. The lean-to sleeps 6, with tent pads for overflow nearby. The shelter is positioned between Siler Bald to the south and Wayah Bald to the north, making it a natural midpoint camp on the Wayah Bald day.

Cold Spring sees moderate traffic — enough to fill the small lean-to on busy weekends, but far less crowded than the shelters near Franklin or the NOC. The surrounding forest is beautiful high-elevation hardwood, with beech and yellow birch dominant. In early summer, wildflowers carpet the forest floor. Fall color here is exceptional, typically peaking in mid-October.

Bear Country: This section of the Nantahala has active black bear populations. Never leave food unattended. Use bear boxes where present, or hang food at least 10 feet high and 4 feet from any vertical surface. Bears here have learned to associate backpacks with food.

Getting Supplies

Cold Spring is a mid-section shelter with no easy road access in either direction. Plan food from Franklin (south) or load up at the NOC (north).

SOUTHBOUND

Franklin, NC via Winding Stair Gap

~11.5 trail miles south to gap, then ~10 road miles west. This is the closest resupply point for those heading south or who need to resupply retroactively. Hike back to Winding Stair Gap (US-64) and hitch to Franklin. For NOBO hikers, this means backtracking — plan food carefully before leaving Franklin so you don't need to return.

NORTHBOUND

Nantahala Outdoor Center (NOC)

~21 trail miles north. The NOC at Wesser is the next resupply northbound. It has an outfitter store with some food, River's End Restaurant, and lodging. The trail from Cold Spring passes Wayah Bald, Rufus Morgan Shelter, and Sassafras Gap before dropping steeply to the NOC at the Nantahala River. Plan 2 days of food minimum from Cold Spring to the NOC.

Food Planning: Leave Franklin with at least 3 days of food if heading northbound. Cold Spring sits between the two major resupply points. There is no road access and no bailout from this shelter.
