Blue Mountain Shelter

Appalachian Trail, Georgia — Mile 50.7 (NOBO) • Lean-to • Elevation ~3,889 ft — Updated 2026

Unicoi Gap (GA-75) is 2.3 miles north — the best road exit in central Georgia. GA-75 runs between Helen (9 mi east) and Hiawassee (11 mi west). Multiple shuttles cover this gap, and hitchhiking is reasonable.

Shelter Overview

AT Mile: 50.7 from Springer
Elevation: 3,889 ft
Capacity: ~7 hikers
Type: Lean-to
Bear Cables: Yes
County: White, GA
Water: Spring nearby, bear cables

Blue Mountain Shelter perches at 3,889 ft on the southern slopes of Blue Mountain (4,020 ft summit), one of the more demanding climbs in the Georgia AT. The shelter is set back slightly from the AT on a blue-blazed side trail and features bear cables — one of the few Georgia shelters with dedicated food storage infrastructure.

From Low Gap Shelter (mile 43.3), hikers climb 7.4 miles through Wildcat Mountain and over Blue Mountain to reach this shelter. It's a demanding day with significant elevation gain. The area from Blue Mountain Shelter down 2.3 miles to Unicoi Gap is a steady rocky descent — take care in wet conditions.

Rocky descent to Unicoi Gap. The 2.3-mile trail from this shelter down to Unicoi Gap (GA-75) is steep and rocky. Use trekking poles and slow down in rain or ice.
Water: Spring source near the shelter, accessible year-round in normal conditions. Use the bear cables — bears in the Blue Mountain area are bold and accustomed to hikers.
Views: Open rocky areas near the Blue Mountain summit (0.1 miles north of shelter) offer good northward views toward Tray Mountain and the Georgia highlands. Worth a 5-minute detour before descending to Unicoi.

Getting Supplies

RECOMMENDED — 2.3 mi north to road

Hiawassee, GA via Unicoi Gap (GA-75)

Hike 2.3 miles north (rocky descent) to Unicoi Gap on GA-75. Head west ~11 miles to Hiawassee — the premier resupply town for north Georgia AT hikers. Ingles Supermarket (full grocery), Trailful Outdoor Co. (gear, food, mail drops at 75 North Main St), post office, laundromat, and multiple restaurants. Free city shuttle from Unicoi Gap during peak season (late Feb–early May). See the Hiawassee shuttle guide.

ALTERNATE — 2.3 mi north + 9 mi east

Helen, GA via Unicoi Gap (GA-75)

From Unicoi Gap, head east on GA-75 ~9 miles to Helen. Grocery store (Ingles), post office, restaurants, and lodging. Closer by road than Hiawassee but smaller supply selection. Unicoi State Park (2 miles NE of Helen) has camping, cabins, showers, laundry, and a restaurant — good for a zero day.

ON-TRAIL — 7.4 mi south

Low Gap Shelter Exit Options

If heading south, Low Gap Shelter (mile 43.3) has access to both Hogpen Gap (GA-348, ~7 mi south) and Unicoi Gap (north). See the Low Gap resupply guide for full details on shuttles from both gaps.

Trailful mail drop address: 75 North Main Street, Hiawassee GA 30546. Include your name, phone number, and approximate arrival date. They will hold boxes for AT hikers. Call (706) 521-HIKE to confirm.
