Birch Spring Gap Shelter

Great Smoky Mountains NP  ·  AT Mile ~168.1  ·  Lean-to  ·  Elev. ~3,400 ft

🏔 Great Smoky Mountains National Park — Permit Required All AT hikers in GSMNP must hold a valid backcountry permit. Purchase at recreation.gov before entering the park. Cost: $40 permit fee + $8/person/night. AT thru-hikers purchase a thru-hiker permit covering their entire transit. Staying at unauthorized locations is a federal offense. Rangers check regularly.

Birch Spring Gap Shelter is the first shelter inside Great Smoky Mountains National Park for NOBO thru-hikers — a milestone that carries serious emotional weight. You crossed Fontana Dam this morning, walked into the park, and climbed Shuckstack Ridge to arrive here. The views from Shuckstack's fire tower (a short scramble off the AT) are your first Smokies panorama: Fontana Lake to the south, the full mass of the Smokies spread ahead of you.

All GSMNP shelters have a chain-link fenced enclosure around the sleeping area — a feature unique to the Smokies. This is not optional. All hikers and their food must sleep inside the enclosure, and the gate must be latched at night. The bears in the Smokies are genuinely habituated to humans and extremely motivated food thieves; the enclosures are non-negotiable. The system works, but it requires discipline from every hiker.

The shelter has a capacity of 12 inside the enclosure, with a bear-proof picnic table area and privy nearby. A spring on a signed blue-blaze side trail provides reliable water. At 3,400 feet, this is the lowest of the Smokies AT shelters — you'll gain significant elevation heading north. The surrounding forest is a beautiful cove hardwood community transitioning to the higher-elevation old-growth the Smokies are famous for.

Shuckstack Fire Tower: 0.3 miles south of Birch Spring Gap on the AT, then a short side trail to the summit. The tower is locked but the base platform offers outstanding 360° views. Worth the detour if you have daylight.

GSMNP Rules — Know Before You Sleep

No dispersed camping in GSMNP. You must stay at a designated shelter or designated backcountry campsite. The AT passes only shelters — not campsites — between Fontana Dam and Davenport Gap. Your permit must list the specific shelter for each night.
All food, trash, and scented items must be inside the bear enclosure when you are in camp. The gate must be latched at all times except when actively entering or exiting. Leave nothing outside.
Permit Must Match Shelter: Your recreation.gov permit lists specific shelter names for specific nights. Rangers check. If you need to change shelters due to weather or injury, contact the park at (865) 436-1231 to modify your permit.

Getting Supplies

SOUTH — LAST EXIT

Fontana Village

~3.5 trail miles south to Fontana Dam, then 2 road miles. The last easy resupply before the Smokies. Fontana Village (300 Woods Rd, Fontana Dam, NC 28733) has a camp store and restaurant. If you didn't fully stock up at Fontana, this is your last chance before committing to the 59-mile traverse to Davenport Gap. You should have 5–6 days of food when entering the Smokies.

NORTH

Gatlinburg, TN via Newfound Gap

~32 trail miles north to Newfound Gap (US-441), then ~15 road miles west. Gatlinburg is the primary resupply midpoint for Smokies thru-hikers, accessed via a hitchhike from Newfound Gap. Plan for 4+ days of food from Birch Spring Gap to Newfound Gap. Gatlinburg has grocery stores, outfitters, and hiker-friendly lodging.

Where to Sleep

Birch Spring Gap Shelter (GSMNP Lean-to) Free (permit req.)
On trail · AT Mile ~168.1 · Permit required · Capacity 12 inside enclosure
Chain-link enclosed lean-to with sleeping platform for 12. All hikers must sleep inside the enclosure; gate must stay latched. Spring water on blue-blaze side trail. Privy on site. At 3,400 ft, this is a relatively warm shelter by Smokies standards. You must reserve this shelter specifically on your recreation.gov permit.
Pros
  • First Smokies shelter — milestone
  • Lower elevation, warmer nights
  • Reliable spring water
  • Privy on site
Cons
  • Permit required and must be reserved in advance
  • Chain-link enclosure can feel confining
  • No cell service
  • If shelter is full and you didn't book, you have a problem
Fontana Village Resort ~$80–140/night
~3.5 trail miles south + 2 road miles · Seasonal
If you need to delay park entry or didn't get your permit sorted, Fontana Village is a comfortable fallback. Lodge rooms, restaurant, camp store. Call ahead: (800) 849-2258. Address: 300 Woods Rd, Fontana Dam, NC 28733.
Pros
  • Private rooms and restaurant
  • Last real lodging before the Smokies traverse
Cons
  • 3.5 miles of backtracking
  • Expensive for resort pricing
