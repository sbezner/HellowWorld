Spence Field Shelter

Great Smoky Mountains National Park — NC/TN Border — AT Mile ~180.7
Great Smoky Mountains NP — Permit Required All AT shelters in GSMNP require a reservation through recreation.gov. Cost: $40 reservation fee + $8 per person per night. Purchase your permit before entering the park — you cannot buy one on the trail. All shelters have chain-link bear enclosures: you must sleep inside the enclosure and the gate must remain latched at all times. No dispersed camping is permitted anywhere in the park. You must stay at the shelter listed on your permit — to change shelters, call (865) 436-1231.

Spence Field Shelter is one of the most celebrated camp spots in the Smokies. It sits adjacent to Spence Field, one of the largest natural grassy balds in the entire Great Smoky Mountains — a sweeping open meadow that provides panoramic views in all directions. On a clear day, Cades Cove is visible to the north in Tennessee, one of the park's most visited and historically significant valleys. To the south and east, the main ridge of the Smokies rolls on in both directions.

The grassy bald is actively maintained by the National Park Service — without periodic mowing and management, the forest would begin reclaiming this meadow within decades, as forest succession naturally fills in open areas at this elevation. The bald is believed to have originated from a combination of Indigenous burning practices, early settlement grazing, and possibly natural factors; its exact origin remains a subject of ecological research.

"Rocky Top": Rocky Top, the famous Tennessee state song (and University of Tennessee fight song), was written by Felice and Boudleaux Bryant in 1967 — inspired by this area of the Smokies. The actual rocky summit of Rocky Top is visible from Spence Field, about 0.5 miles east on the main ridge. Many hikers make the short detour to stand on the summit celebrated in song.

GSMNP Rules at a Glance

Permit required before entering: Book at recreation.gov ($40 + $8/person/night). You cannot legally camp here without a valid permit for this specific shelter.
  • Sleep inside the chain-link bear enclosure — no exceptions.
  • Keep the enclosure gate latched at all times, even while entering or exiting.
  • No dispersed camping anywhere in Great Smoky Mountains NP.
  • Stay only at the shelter listed on your permit. To change shelters, call (865) 436-1231.
  • Campfires only in established fire rings where permitted — check current conditions for fire bans.
  • Stay on designated trails — the bald meadow vegetation is fragile; do not trample off-trail.

Getting Supplies

Spence Field is at mile ~180.7 with no in-park resupply available. You are approximately 19+ trail miles from Newfound Gap and the Gatlinburg hitch northbound.

Fontana Village, NC

Recommended — Pre-Entry Resupply

The essential resupply point before entering GSMNP from the south. Fontana Village has a camp store, restaurant, and hiker services. You are roughly 11 trail miles north of Fontana Dam from here — not practical to backtrack mid-traverse, but critical for pre-entry planning. Carry sufficient food before you start.

Gatlinburg, TN

Alternate — Northbound Exit

Gatlinburg is your next resupply northbound — accessible from Newfound Gap via a hitch on US-441 (~15 road miles west). From Spence Field, you are approximately 19+ trail miles from Newfound Gap. Plan 4 days of food to cover this stretch. Gatlinburg has full grocery (Food City, Walmart), outfitters, and multiple hiker lodging options.

No resupply inside the park. Spence Field is roughly the midpoint of the Smokies AT traverse. You should be tracking your food supply carefully — you need enough to make it another 19+ miles to Newfound Gap.
