Day Hike Guide

Neel Gap heading north — Appalachian Trail, Georgia

Overview

Start: Neel Gap (Mile 31.7)
End: Unicoi Gap (Mile 43.8)
Distance: ~12 miles
Elevation Gain: ~3,000 ft
Difficulty: Strenuous

Mile by Mile

Neel Gap / Walasi-Yi (Mile 31.7)

Starting point

The only building the AT passes through. Stock up at Mountain Crossings outfitter — food, gear, and advice available. Fill water here before heading out.

Climb out of Neel Gap

Miles 31.7 – 34

The trail climbs steeply immediately after the gap. This is one of the harder early-Georgia climbs — expect sustained uphill through dense forest with rocky footing. Take it slow if legs are tired from Blood Mountain the day before.

Cowrock Mountain (3,842 ft)

Mile ~34.5

First major summit of the day. The trail rolls over the top with partial views through the trees. A short scramble near the top rewards with open rock outcrops.

Tesnatee Gap (Hwy 348)

Mile ~36.5

Road crossing and brief descent. No services, but an emergency exit if needed. The gap sits at 3,138 ft — enjoy the flat before the next climb up Wildcat Mountain.

Wildcat Mountain

Mile ~38

Another stiff climb after the gap. The trail is relentlessly rolling through here — no long flat sections. Good views open up near the top on clear days.

Low Gap Shelter (water)

Mile ~42

A reliable spring and lean-to shelter make this a great lunch or rest stop. About halfway through the day's miles. The spring is 0.1 miles off trail — worth the detour to refill.

Blue Mountain (4,025 ft)

Mile ~43

The final significant climb of the day. At over 4,000 ft, the summit can be socked in with clouds on wet days. Blue Mountain Shelter sits just beyond the summit — a good bailout if energy is low.

Unicoi Gap (Hwy 75)

Mile 43.8

End of the day's hike. The trail descends steeply to this road crossing at 2,949 ft. Helen, GA is 8 miles east — a popular trail town for food, lodging, and resupply. Shuttles are often available from here.

What to Expect

  • Terrain: Constant ups and downs — no long flat sections. Rocky footing throughout.
  • Water: Limited sources between Neel Gap and Low Gap Shelter. Carry at least 2 liters out of Neel Gap.
  • Weather: Mornings often foggy and cold at elevation, even in spring. Afternoon thunderstorms possible in summer.
  • Wildlife: Black bears are active in this section — hang food or use a bear canister.
  • Cell service: Spotty or none for most of this section. Download offline maps before starting.
Tip: If your legs are sore from Blood Mountain, consider breaking this into two days — Low Gap Shelter to Unicoi Gap is only 2 miles and makes an easy second morning.
