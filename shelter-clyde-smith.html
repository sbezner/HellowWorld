SHELTER CLOSED: Clyde Smith Shelter is currently closed by the Cherokee National Forest due to food-conditioned bears in the area. Do NOT plan to camp at or near this shelter. The closure affects the shelter and the immediate surrounding area. Check with the U.S. Forest Service for current closure status before your hike.

Clyde Smith Shelter is named for Clyde Smith, a prolific AT sign maker who contributed countless trail markers and signs along the Appalachian Trail in Tennessee. The shelter sits approximately 120 yards off the AT on a blue-blazed side trail at 4,400 feet elevation in Carter County, Tennessee.

The shelter is a wood frame lean-to with a metal roof and a covered porch area. It features upper and lower bunks sleeping 10. The water source is a spring located 205 yards west of the shelter down a steep hillside — the water collects in a buried drum and flows through a 2-inch pipe. The descent to the spring is steep and can be slippery when wet.

Clyde Smith Shelter is maintained by the Tennessee Eastman Hiking and Canoeing Club (TEHCC). Due to the current bear closure, hikers should plan to camp elsewhere. The next shelter north is Roan High Knob Shelter (also currently closed for structural repairs) — check trail conditions carefully and have alternate camping plans for this section.

Bear Safety: This shelter was closed due to bears that have become habituated to human food. Even when the closure is lifted, practice strict bear safety: hang food bags properly or use a bear canister, cook and eat well away from your sleeping area, and never leave food unattended.

Getting Supplies

Clyde Smith is between Erwin and Roan Mountain. The closest resupply option heading NOBO is Roan Mountain, TN, accessible from US 19E.

AHEAD

Roan Mountain, TN — ~12 trail miles north

~12 trail miles north via the AT to US 19E, then 3 miles west. Roan Mountain is a small town with a Dollar General for basic resupply and a few restaurants. Hitching from US 19E is common and usually quick. Mountain Harbour B&B near Carvers Gap (~10 trail miles north) also offers limited resupply items.
