Mollies Ridge Shelter

Great Smoky Mountains National Park — NC/TN Border — AT Mile ~177.8
Great Smoky Mountains NP — Permit Required All AT shelters in GSMNP require a reservation through recreation.gov. Cost: $40 reservation fee + $8 per person per night. Purchase your permit before entering the park — you cannot buy one on the trail. All shelters have chain-link bear enclosures: you must sleep inside the enclosure and the gate must remain latched at all times. No dispersed camping is permitted anywhere in the park. You must stay at the shelter listed on your permit — to change shelters, call (865) 436-1231.

Mollies Ridge is a classic Smokies ridge camp, positioned on the long spine of the main divide between Russell Field and the bald country at Spence Field. The shelter sits in a forested setting with partial views through the trees — quieter and more intimate than the open bald shelters on either side of it. It sees moderate traffic and is a reliable mid-section stop.

The shelter is named for Mollie Price, a Cherokee woman whose family lived in this area before the park was established in 1934. The creation of Great Smoky Mountains National Park displaced over 6,000 people from their homes — many of them Cherokee and Appalachian farm families who had lived here for generations. That history is woven into the names and landscape of the ridge you walk.

Historical context: Mollies Ridge is one of several features in the Smokies named for people who lived here before the park displaced them. As you walk this ridge, you are crossing land that was farmed, hunted, and inhabited long before it became a national park. The Cherokee called these mountains Shaconage — "land of blue smoke."

GSMNP Rules at a Glance

Permit required before entering: Book at recreation.gov ($40 + $8/person/night). You cannot legally camp here without a valid permit for this specific shelter.
  • Sleep inside the chain-link bear enclosure — no exceptions.
  • Keep the enclosure gate latched at all times, even while entering or exiting.
  • No dispersed camping anywhere in Great Smoky Mountains NP.
  • Stay only at the shelter listed on your permit. To change shelters, call (865) 436-1231.
  • Campfires only in established fire rings where permitted — check current conditions for fire bans.
  • Pack out all trash; leave no trace standards apply throughout the park.

Getting Supplies

Mollies Ridge is at mile ~177.8, well within the Smokies corridor with no in-park resupply available. You are roughly 22+ trail miles from Newfound Gap and a Gatlinburg hitch northbound.

Fontana Village, NC

Recommended — Pre-Entry Resupply

The primary resupply point before entering the park from the south. Fontana Village has a camp store, restaurant, and laundry. Most northbound hikers stock up here for the full Smokies carry. From Mollies Ridge, Fontana is roughly 8 trail miles back south — not practical mid-hike, but essential pre-entry planning.

Gatlinburg, TN

Alternate — Northbound Exit

Gatlinburg is the next major resupply northbound, via a hitch from Newfound Gap (US-441, ~15 road miles west). From Mollies Ridge you are approximately 22+ trail miles from Newfound Gap. Plan at least 4 days of food ahead. Gatlinburg has Food City, Walmart, outfitters, and hiker-friendly lodging options.

No resupply inside the park. There are zero stores, vending machines, or resupply options between Fontana Dam and Newfound Gap. Build your food carry before entering.
