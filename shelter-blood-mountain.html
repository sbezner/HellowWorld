Shelter Overview

AT Mile: 31.7 from Springer
Elevation: ~4,461 ft
Capacity: ~8 hikers
Type: Historic Stone Cabin (CCC, 1930s)
County: Union, GA

Blood Mountain Shelter is the most iconic shelter in Georgia — a two-room stone cabin built by the Civilian Conservation Corps in the 1930s, perched near the summit of Blood Mountain at 4,461 ft, the highest point on the AT in Georgia. The cabin is solid stone with a plank floor and two separate rooms. It has weathered nearly a century of Appalachian storms and remains a proud landmark on the southern AT.

The summit offers sweeping 360-degree views on clear days — one of the best viewpoints in the Georgia AT. On clear nights, the lights of the Atlanta metro can sometimes be seen to the south. The cabin walls are thick and provide significant wind protection, but interior temperatures can still drop sharply on exposed nights.

NO WATER at the shelter. The nearest reliable water source is approximately 0.5 miles south back down the AT, near Slaughter Creek. This is one of the most critical planning points for Blood Mountain — carry adequate water from Slaughter Creek before the final climb to the summit.
No fires allowed. Open fires are prohibited in and around the historic cabin. Leave No Trace strictly enforced by USFS rangers who regularly patrol this high-use area.
Very high use. Blood Mountain sees significant day-hiker traffic from Neel Gap and Byron Reece trailheads. On weekends, the summit and shelter can be extremely crowded. Arrive early or plan to camp elsewhere.
Views: The open granite summit just north of the cabin has panoramic views. In October and early November, fall foliage on surrounding ridges is spectacular. The name "Blood Mountain" comes from a Cherokee legend about a battle that turned the mountain red with blood.

Getting Supplies

ON THE TRAIL — 1.1 miles north

Mountain Crossings at Neel Gap (Walasi-Yi Center)

The AT passes directly through the building — the only place on the entire 2,190-mile trail where this happens. Mountain Crossings stocks hiker food, fuel, gear, stove parts, water filters, maps, and apparel. Expert gear advice and free pack shakedowns (they've thrown hundreds of pounds of unnecessary gear over the years). No shuttle or road crossing required.

Call 706-745-6095 mountaincrossings.com
FULL SERVICES — 13 mi north by road

Blairsville, GA via US-19/129 N from Neel Gap

Hike to Neel Gap (1.1 miles north) and hitch or shuttle north on US-19/129 to Blairsville (~13 miles). Full grocery (Ingles), pharmacy, hardware, restaurants, and chain motels. Better for a full resupply box than Mountain Crossings alone. Shuttles available via Mountain Crossings or local services.

PRE/POST HIKE — 17 mi south by road

Dahlonega, GA via US-19/129 S from Neel Gap

17 miles south of Neel Gap. Walmart, full grocery, outfitters, restaurants. Best used for staging before your hike or for a full resupply if heading to Blairsville isn't convenient. Hitch at Neel Gap — US-19/129 has good traffic in both directions.

Hitchhiking from Neel Gap: US-19/129 (Gainesville Highway) carries steady traffic. Stand at the Mountain Crossings parking lot exit. Drivers heading north toward Blairsville or south toward Dahlonega stop regularly for hikers. Average wait: 10–20 minutes in season.

Where to Sleep Nearby

Mountain Crossings at Neel Gap — Bunk Room
~$25–35/night ON THE TRAIL
12471 US-19 N (Walasi-Yi Center), Blairsville, GA 30512 — 1.1 miles north on the AT
The most convenient lodging option for Blood Mountain — literally on the AT. The bunk room is basic (dormitory style) but includes hot showers and laundry access. A spot here means you can resupply, gear-fix, and rest all in one place without any road travel. Fills up fast in season — call or arrive early.
Pros
  • Zero road travel required
  • Cheapest option near Blood Mountain
  • Resupply, gear repair, showers all on-site
  • Great hiker community hub
Cons
  • Very basic — dormitory bunks only
  • Limited privacy
  • Fills up early in peak season
  • No restaurant on site
Call 706-745-6095 mountaincrossings.com
Blood Mountain Cabins
~$90–150/night
Near Neel Gap, Cleveland, GA area — ~2 miles east of Neel Gap by road
Private cabins just off US-129 near Neel Gap. A step up from the bunk room — actual privacy, a real bed, and cabin amenities. Popular for section hikers or couples. No food provided but Neel Gap (Mountain Crossings) is within a short drive. Call Mountain Crossings for current recommendations as cabin availability in this area fluctuates.
Pros
  • Private room/cabin — real rest
  • Close to Neel Gap
  • Good for couples or solo hikers wanting quiet
Cons
  • Requires road transportation
  • More expensive than bunk room
  • No meals included
Hotels in Blairsville, GA
~$80–140/night
Blairsville, GA — ~13 miles north of Neel Gap on US-19/129
Blairsville has chain hotels (Holiday Inn Express, Quality Inn) and local motels. It's a small mountain town with full grocery (Ingles), restaurants, and a pharmacy. Shuttles from Neel Gap available via Mountain Crossings. Good choice if you want a proper bed, a restaurant meal, and a solid resupply before continuing north.
Pros
  • Full services — grocery, pharmacy, restaurants
  • Comfortable hotel rooms
  • Quieter than hiker hostel
Cons
  • 13 miles from Neel Gap by road
  • Less hiker-community feel
  • Shuttle cost adds up
