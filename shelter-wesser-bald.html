Wesser Bald Shelter

North Carolina  ·  AT Mile ~131.2  ·  Lean-to  ·  Elev. ~4,115 ft

Wesser Bald Fire Tower — Second CCC Tower on This Section Wesser Bald Shelter sits near the summit of Wesser Bald, which features another restored fire tower with panoramic views — similar to the Wayah Bald CCC tower 15.8 miles south. On clear days the views encompass Wayah Bald, Albert Mountain, the Cheoah Range, Rocky Top, Thunderhead, and Clingmans Dome to the north. Sunsets from this tower are exceptional.

Shelter Overview

AT Mile ~131.2
Elevation ~4,115 ft
Capacity 6
Type Lean-to (Nantahala style)

Wesser Bald Shelter holds the distinction of being the first shelter built in the Nantahala style — a design developed by the Nantahala Hiking Club in the early 1990s and constructed here in the summer of 1994. The Nantahala style features a more enclosed, weather-resistant design compared to classic open lean-tos, with improved protection from the rain and wind that frequently sweep the high ridges of this section. The shelter sits approximately 100 yards left (west) of the AT when heading northbound.

The water source is a beautifully constructed boxed spring located 0.1 miles south of the shelter, then 75 yards down a blue-blaze side trail. This spring was originally developed to supply water to the fire tower on Wesser Bald summit — a reliable, high-quality source maintained over decades. Plenty of tent sites are spread across the west side of the gap, accommodating overflow on busy nights.

Wesser Bald itself (4,627 ft at summit) sits just above the shelter and hosts a fire tower similar to the one on Wayah Bald — though less well-known and therefore less crowded. The tower provides extraordinary views in every direction, including the full sweep of the Nantahala highlands you've just crossed and a first glimpse northward of the terrain ahead: the steep Cheoah Mountains dropping to the Nantahala Gorge, and beyond that the wall of the Great Smoky Mountains on the horizon.

Wesser Bald Tower vs. Wayah Bald Tower: Both are CCC-era fire towers with exceptional views. Wayah gets more traffic due to the USFS road access. Wesser Bald is foot-traffic only — you'll often have the tower entirely to yourself. Worth the short climb from the shelter.

Getting Supplies

Wesser Bald is 2.8 miles from the NOC — close enough that most hikers continue to the NOC for resupply rather than stopping here. This is one of the few shelters on the AT where resupply is within arm's reach to the north.

RECOMMENDED

Nantahala Outdoor Center (NOC) — 2.8 miles north

2.8 trail miles north on AT. The NOC at Wesser is a short morning hike from Wesser Bald Shelter. The Wesser Store carries outfitter supplies and some food; River's End Restaurant serves hot meals. Dorm lodging (~$35–45/bunk) and private rooms (~$90–150) are available. Many hikers use Wesser Bald as their last night before the NOC, arriving mid-morning in time for breakfast at the restaurant. See the NOC dedicated page for full details. Mail drop: 13077 US-19 W, Bryson City, NC 28713.

SOUTHBOUND

Franklin, NC via Winding Stair Gap

~29.7 trail miles south to Winding Stair Gap, then ~10 road miles west. Franklin is well behind you at this point. If you're heading southbound (SOBO) or need a major resupply that the NOC can't provide, Franklin has Ingles Supermarket and Outdoor 76 outfitter (74 E Main St). Not practical for NOBO hikers — the NOC is the obvious choice.

NOC Mail Drop: Nantahala Outdoor Center, 13077 US-19 W, Bryson City, NC 28713. If you're just 2.8 miles away, you could have shipped a box here and pick it up at the NOC tomorrow morning.

Where to Sleep Nearby

Wesser Bald Shelter (AT Lean-to) Free
On trail · AT Mile ~131.2 · Open year-round · Capacity 6 + tenting
The original Nantahala-style lean-to, built 1994. Well-constructed with better weather protection than classic open lean-tos. Boxed spring 0.1 mi south on blue blaze — excellent water quality. Ample tent sites on the west side of the gap. Privy maintained. Bear cables present. At 4,115 ft, the shelter is at moderate elevation — nights are cool but rarely frigid. An excellent last-night shelter before arriving at the NOC the next morning.
Pros
  • Free, no reservation
  • Excellent boxed spring water
  • Nantahala-style: better weather protection
  • Fire tower nearby with panoramic views
  • Only 2.8 miles from the NOC
Cons
  • Small capacity (6)
  • Water source 0.1 mi away
  • No cell service
  • Mice in shelter
NOC Basecamp Dorm — Wesser, NC ~$35–45/bunk
2.8 trail miles north · Year-round
The NOC's bunk-style dorm is 2.8 miles north — a short morning hike. Hot showers, laundry, River's End Restaurant steps away. Budget option on the NOC campus. Many hikers stay at Wesser Bald Shelter tonight and arrive at the NOC in time for a full breakfast. See the NOC page for complete details.
Pros
  • Very close — 2.8 miles
  • Hot showers and laundry
  • Restaurant on campus
  • Outfitter for resupply
Cons
  • Costs money (~$35–45/bunk)
  • Shared dorm facilities
  • Can be crowded on weekends
NOC Lodge / Private Rooms — Wesser, NC ~$90–150/night
2.8 trail miles north · Year-round (reserve ahead)
Private lodge rooms and cabins on the NOC campus overlooking the Nantahala Gorge. Worth the splurge if you want a real bed and private bathroom before the demanding Cheoah Mountains section that follows the NOC. Reserve ahead — especially busy spring through fall.
Pros
  • Private room — real bed
  • Scenic gorge location
  • All NOC amenities accessible
Cons
  • Most expensive option
  • Reserve well in advance
  • Still 2.8 miles away
