Deep Gap Shelter

Appalachian Trail, Georgia — Mile 63.3 (NOBO) • Lean-to • Elevation ~3,550 ft — Updated 2026

Dick's Creek Gap (US-76) is 6.1 miles north — the best hitchhiking gap in Georgia. US-76 is the busiest highway crossing on the Georgia AT. Hiawassee is 11 miles west, Clayton is 13 miles east.

Shelter Overview

AT Mile: 63.3 from Springer
Elevation: ~3,550 ft
Capacity: ~8 hikers
Type: Lean-to
County: Towns/Rabun, GA

Deep Gap Shelter occupies a forested gap between Wolfpen Ridge and Kelly Knob, approximately 6.1 miles south of Dick's Creek Gap (US-76). It's one of the less-celebrated Georgia shelters but sits at a comfortable mid-elevation with good flat tent sites and reliable water. For NOBO thru-hikers, Deep Gap is a strategic stopping point — close enough to Dick's Creek Gap to push on and reach Hiawassee for a town stop.

The surrounding forest of hardwoods and hemlocks is classic Appalachian corridor. The gap itself is relatively sheltered from wind compared to higher-elevation sites in this section. This shelter sees moderate traffic — more than Tray Mountain but far less than the Blood Mountain / Neel Gap corridor.

Active bear area. This section between Tray Mountain and Dick's Creek Gap sees regular bear activity. Hang food properly — no bear cables reported at this shelter. Use a bear canister or hang at least 10 ft high, 4 ft from the trunk.
Water: Spring nearby, generally reliable. Check recent FarOut (Guthook) comments for current conditions, especially in August–September when springs can run low.

Getting Supplies

RECOMMENDED — 6.1 mi north to US-76

Hiawassee, GA via Dick's Creek Gap (US-76)

Hike 6.1 miles north to Dick's Creek Gap on US-76 — the best hitchhiking spot in Georgia. Good shoulder, heavy traffic. Stand on the right side heading west. Hiawassee (11 miles west) has everything: Ingles Supermarket, Trailful Outdoor Co. (gear + mail drops), post office, pharmacy, laundromat, and restaurants including the well-liked Smoke Ring BBQ. Free city shuttle runs during peak season from Dick's Creek Gap to Ingles. See the full Hiawassee guide.

ALTERNATE — 6.1 mi north + 13 mi east

Clayton, GA via Dick's Creek Gap (US-76 East)

From Dick's Creek Gap, head east on US-76 (~13 miles) to Clayton, GA — a growing mountain town with a grocery store, outfitter (Sunburst Trails), restaurants, and lodging. Clayton has less hiker infrastructure than Hiawassee but a nicer town feel and good food. Hitchhiking east from Dick's Creek Gap is slower than west toward Hiawassee.

SKIP AHEAD — 5.1 mi north on trail

Plumorchard Gap Shelter — Mile 68.4

If you have enough food, push 5.1 miles north to Plumorchard Gap Shelter — just 1 mile south of Dick's Creek Gap. You'll be set up for a morning exit to Hiawassee or Clayton. Not a supply option by itself, but a strategic overnight position for an early town stop the next day.

Hitchhiking at Dick's Creek Gap: US-76 carries the most traffic of any gap on the Georgia AT. Average wait is 10–25 minutes westbound to Hiawassee. The city free shuttle (peak season only) stops here morning and midday. Trailful in Hiawassee keeps a current shuttle list — call (706) 521-HIKE.
