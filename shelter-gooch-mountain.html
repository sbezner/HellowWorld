Gooch Mountain Shelter

Appalachian Trail, Georgia — Mile 15.8 (NOBO) • Lean-to • Elevation ~2,784 ft — Updated 2026

Road access at Woody Gap (GA-60) — 2.8 miles north on trail. This is the most accessible shelter in the first 20 miles of the Georgia AT. Hitch or shuttle to Suches (1.5 miles west on GA-60) or Dahlonega (~13 miles south on GA-60/US-19).

Shelter Overview

AT Mile: 15.8 from Springer
Elevation: ~2,784 ft
Capacity: ~8 hikers
Type: Lean-to
County: Dawson/Lumpkin, GA

Gooch Mountain Shelter is roughly halfway between Springer Mountain and Neel Gap, making it a popular stopping point for 3-day NOBO hikers heading to their first town stop. The shelter sits on a somewhat exposed ridgeline near the summit of Gooch Mountain at a relatively modest 2,784 ft — lower than many Georgia shelters. The surrounding forest provides good wind protection but less dramatic views.

Woody Gap (GA-60) is just 2.8 miles ahead — making this shelter the first practical road-accessible bail-out or resupply point in the southern Georgia AT. Many early-season NOBO hikers make it to Gooch Mountain on Day 2 or 3 and either push on to Neel Gap or slip out to town for a night.

Active bear corridor. Black bears are very active between Springer and Neel Gap. Use the food hang cables or a bear canister. Several hiker reports of bear encounters near this shelter in recent seasons.
Water: Spring east of the shelter, approximately 50–100 yards on the blue-blazed trail. Reliable year-round in normal conditions.

Getting Supplies

BEST OPTION — 2.8 mi north + hitch

Suches, GA via Woody Gap (GA-60)

Hike 2.8 miles north to Woody Gap. Turn left (west) on GA-60 and walk or hitch 1.5 miles to Suches. The Wolf Pen Gap Country Store in Suches has basic supplies, beer, snacks, and sometimes hot food. Very limited but real supplies. Woody Gap has a small parking area with moderate traffic — hitching is possible. Dahlonega is 13 miles south from Woody Gap.

BEST FULL RESUPPLY — 15.9 trail mi ahead

Mountain Crossings at Neel Gap — Mile 31.7

Continue 15.9 miles north through the Ramrock Mountain area and Blood Mountain to reach the iconic Mountain Crossings outfitter at Neel Gap. Full resupply, gear, fuel, and a bunk room. If you are well-supplied and healthy, this is the preferred first resupply — no backtracking needed.

FULL SERVICES — 13 mi by road

Dahlonega, GA via Woody Gap & GA-60/US-19

From Woody Gap, hitchhike south ~13 miles on GA-60 to Dahlonega. Full grocery, Walmart, restaurants, pharmacies, and outfitters. Worth a trip if you need to replace gear or want a real resupply. Call ahead for a shuttle rather than hitching — GA-60 has moderate traffic.

Hitchhiking at Woody Gap: GA-60 is lightly trafficked compared to US-76 (Dick's Creek Gap) later in the trail, but hikers are picked up regularly. Weekend traffic is better. Hold a sign or just start walking and stick your thumb out. Average wait: 20–45 minutes.

Where to Sleep Nearby

Hiker Hostel (Suches)
~$30–40 bunk / ~$75 private
7693 Hiker Hostel Way, Suches, GA 30572 — ~4.3 miles from Woody Gap (1.5 mi to Suches + 2.8 mi back to shelter = shuttle from Woody Gap recommended)
The Hiker Hostel in Suches is the closest hostel to Gooch Mountain — about 4–5 miles total via Woody Gap. It offers a genuine AT hiker experience with bunk rooms, private cabins, laundry, hot showers, and a kitchen. Free tent camping available. Known for its welcoming owners who have been part of the hiker community for years. Shuttle pickup at Woody Gap available if you call ahead.

Hotels in Dahlonega
~$80–160/night
Dahlonega, GA — ~13 miles south of Woody Gap on GA-60/US-19
Dahlonega has several hotels and B&Bs. The Barefoot Hills Hotel (hiker-friendly, gear drying room, downtown location) and various chain motels on US-19 are popular options. Full grocery (Ingles), restaurants, and the University of North Georgia give the town energy and good food options.
Pros
  • Full Walmart and grocery
  • Multiple restaurant choices
  • University town vibe — fun to visit
  • Gear shop (Black Bear Adventures)
Cons
  • 13 miles by road from Woody Gap
  • More expensive than hostel
  • No direct trail access — shuttle needed
