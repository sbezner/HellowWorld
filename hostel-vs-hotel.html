Green Dragon Hostel vs. Hotel

Deciding where to stay near the AT in Hiawassee / Helen, Georgia — Updated March 2026

On solitude vs. the hostel experience: The Green Dragon is unusually quiet for a hostel — privacy curtains, memory foam bunks, and a "super dark, super quiet" room. If you want to dip your toes into the AT hostel culture without giving up sleep or privacy, it's the best gateway. But if you truly need your own space to recharge, a private cabin at Unicoi State Park is the move — surrounded by forest, lake views, and silence.

Quick Decision Guide

Choose Green Dragon if you...

  • Want the full AT thru-hiker community experience
  • Value laundry, hot breakfast, and shuttle all-in-one
  • Plan to slackpack and return each night
  • Are on a budget ($60/night all-inclusive)
  • Want to push yourself to meet people on trail
  • Love Lord of the Rings 🧙

Choose a hotel/cabin if you...

  • Need true solitude to decompress
  • Prefer your own bathroom and space
  • Are section hiking and not in "thru-hiker mode"
  • Want nature surroundings (Unicoi State Park)
  • Don't need an AT-specific shuttle
  • Willing to pay more for privacy
AT HOSTEL

Green Dragon Hostel

Hiawassee, GA — Free pickup at Unicoi Gap & Dick's Creek Gap
$60/night + tax — all-inclusive
Created by former educators Donna and Bill, the Green Dragon is named after the Lord of the Rings and has become the most popular AT hostel in Georgia. Praised overwhelmingly in 2024–2025 trail journals for its warm hosts, surprisingly quiet bunks, and community feel. Two resident cats and the legendary "Bricky" (carried the entire AT in 2024) add to the charm.
"Owners Donna & Bill, along with staff member Nimrod, are some of the finest people I've met not just on trail, but anywhere." — 2025 AT thru-hiker, The Trek
"Super dark, super quiet & super air conditioned — each bunk has its own light and privacy curtains." — Green Dragon Hostel description, verified 2025
Pros
  • Free shuttle to/from Unicoi Gap
  • Privacy curtains on every bunk
  • Memory foam mattresses
  • Laundry done for you
  • Hot breakfast (all dietary needs)
  • Slackpacking + day packs loaned
  • Coffee all day, WiFi, streaming
  • Only $60 all-inclusive
  • Loaner clothes while laundry runs
  • 4 miles to grocery, pharmacy, restaurants
Cons for solitude seekers
  • Shared bunk room — not truly private
  • Shared bathrooms
  • Social atmosphere — other hikers present
  • No private room option noted
  • Peak season can fill up fast

BEST FOR SOLITUDE

Unicoi State Park & Lodge

1788 Highway 356, Helen, GA 30545 — 2 miles from Helen, ~9 miles from Unicoi Gap
Cabins from ~$120–$200/night — check booking.com for rates
Set in the Chattahoochee National Forest around Lake Unicoi. The quietest, most private option near this section of the AT. Rustic cabins with balconies overlooking the lake, full kitchens, and complete solitude. On-site restaurant with stone fireplace. No AT shuttle — you'll need to arrange your own transport to/from Unicoi Gap.

HOTEL

Holiday Inn Express & Suites Helen

Helen, GA — ~9 miles east of Unicoi Gap on GA-75
~$120–$180/night — check IHG or Hotels.com for rates
Rated 4.7/5 — the highest-rated standard hotel in the Helen area. Private rooms, pool, and predictable chain-hotel comfort. Good base if you want your own space but don't need the wilderness vibe of Unicoi State Park. No AT-specific shuttle but Helen is right on GA-75 making Unicoi Gap hitchable or rideshare-accessible.
Pros
  • Highest-rated hotel in Helen (4.7/5)
  • Private room and bathroom
  • Pool
  • In-town location — restaurants walkable
  • Reliable chain quality
  • Free breakfast (IHG standard)
Cons
  • No AT shuttle
  • No hiker-specific amenities
  • No laundry service
  • Touristic town vibe — less trail atmosphere

HOSTEL ALTERNATIVE

Stanimal's Around the Bend

2708 Highway 17, Hiawassee, GA — AT Mile 52.7, 7.4 miles west of Unicoi Gap
$60/night bunk • Private queen room available • Private cabin available
Run by Stanimal, a 2004 AT thru-hiker. Offers a private queen room and a semi-private cabin — making it a middle-ground option between hostel and hotel. Free pickup at Unicoi Gap for guests. Good for those who want AT culture and shuttle convenience but with more space than a bunk room.
Pros
  • Private room and cabin options
  • Free Unicoi Gap shuttle
  • Community dinner + hot breakfast
  • Laundry included
  • Slackpacking offered
  • AT thru-hiker owned & operated
Cons
  • Private room costs more than bunk
  • Still a hostel atmosphere overall
  • Book ahead in peak season
