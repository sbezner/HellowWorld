Siler Bald Shelter

North Carolina  ·  AT Mile ~107.7  ·  Lean-to  ·  Elev. ~5,240 ft

Exposed High-Elevation Location: Siler Bald is one of the highest points on the AT in this section. The open grassy bald summit is exposed to weather from all sides. Afternoon thunderstorms are frequent in summer — leave the summit before noon if conditions look uncertain. Snow and ice are possible October through April.

Siler Bald Shelter is tucked just below the open grassy summit of Siler Bald (5,216 ft), one of the most scenic natural balds on the southern AT. The summit itself is an open meadow with 360-degree panoramic views — north into the deep Nantahala range, south back over the ridges you've crossed since Georgia, and west toward the Great Smoky Mountains on clear days. It's one of those locations that makes the hard miles feel worthwhile.

The lean-to is set slightly below and east of the bald summit in a sheltered tree line — intentionally positioned to protect campers from the exposed weather that hammers the summit. This is wise: the bald regularly takes the full force of afternoon thunderstorms, and the tree line is the only shelter for a quarter mile in any direction. The spring is located 0.2 miles below the shelter on a side trail and runs reliably, though it should always be filtered.

Siler Bald sits roughly 6 miles north of Winding Stair Gap and is a common first-night camp for hikers leaving Franklin after a resupply. The climb from the gap is steady but not brutal. The bald summit is especially magical at sunset — if you can arrange your timing to be here in the evening, the light show over the Nantahala Wilderness is extraordinary.

Sunset Viewing: The open summit of Siler Bald offers some of the best sunset views in the southern AT. Bring dinner to the summit and watch the light change over the mountains. Leave well before dark to navigate back to the shelter safely.

Getting Supplies

RECOMMENDED

Franklin, NC via Winding Stair Gap (US-64)

~6 trail miles south to gap, then ~10 road miles west. Hike back south 6 miles to Winding Stair Gap on US-64. Hitchhike west to Franklin. This is the standard resupply for Siler Bald — most hikers stop in Franklin before climbing to the bald rather than after. If you're heading north and need resupply, you've passed the main opportunity. Consider whether you have enough food to reach the NOC (~26 miles north via Cold Spring, Wayah Bald, and other shelters).

NORTHBOUND OPTION

Nantahala Outdoor Center (NOC)

~26 trail miles north. The NOC at Wesser is the next major resupply point north of Franklin. It has a full outfitter store (gear and some food), River's End Restaurant, and dorm lodging. The stretch from Siler Bald to the NOC passes Cold Spring Gap, Wayah Bald, and Wayah Shelter — plan food accordingly for 2–3 days of hiking.

Planning Note: If you're NOBO and coming from Franklin, load up with 3+ days of food before leaving Winding Stair Gap. The next reliable resupply after that is the NOC, 32+ trail miles north.

Siler Bald Shelter (AT Lean-to) Free
On trail · AT Mile ~107.7 · Open year-round · Capacity 6 + tenting
Compact lean-to just below the summit bald, sheltered from the worst weather. The summit is magnificent but exposed — do not camp on the open bald. Use bear cables on site. Spring water 0.2 miles downhill on a side trail. At 5,240 ft, this is among the highest shelters in the southern AT — cold at night in any season, potentially frigid in winter and spring.
