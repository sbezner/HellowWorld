Cosby Knob Shelter

Great Smoky Mountains NP  ·  AT Mile ~222.4  ·  Lean-to  ·  Elev. ~4,700 ft

Second-to-Last Smokies Shelter — 4.9 Miles to Davenport Gap Cosby Knob is one camp away from exiting Great Smoky Mountains National Park. Davenport Gap is 4.9 trail miles ahead, and the next major trail town — Hot Springs, NC — is roughly 30 more miles beyond.
🏔 Great Smoky Mountains National Park — Permit Required Purchase at recreation.gov before entering the park. Cost: $40 + $8/person/night. Must stay at the shelter listed on your permit. Gate on bear enclosure must stay latched. Call (865) 436-1231 to modify reservations.

Shelter Overview

AT Mile ~222.4
Elevation ~4,700 ft
Capacity 12
Type Lean-to (chain-link enclosure)

Cosby Knob Shelter sits above the Cosby area of GSMNP — the park's least-visited developed area, famous among those who know it for its quiet campground, old-growth trees, and abundant wildlife. The AT passes the ridge above Cosby Valley on its way to Davenport Gap, and this shelter sits at an elevation drop-off point where the high-elevation spruce-fir begins to give way to the lower hardwood forest of the park's northeastern corner.

At 4,700 feet, Cosby Knob is at a transition elevation — red spruce still appear here, but mixed hardwoods (beech, yellow birch, sugar maple) increasingly dominate. The forest is beautiful and less damaged by woolly adelgid than the pure fir stands higher up. A reliable spring provides water near the shelter. Privy on site. The terrain from here to Davenport Gap descends significantly — you'll lose over 2,700 feet in the final 4.9 miles.

Wildlife in the Cosby area is exceptional. The eastern Smokies are considered some of the best black bear habitat in the eastern US, and the quieter Cosby section sees bears regularly. The area also has historic significance: before the park was established, extensive logging operations cleared much of this terrain. The forest you see today is 80–100 years old second growth, and its recovery is one of the great conservation success stories of the 20th century.

The Final Push: From Cosby Knob, it's 4.9 miles to Davenport Gap and the park boundary. You'll descend steeply off the main ridge into the gap. Hot Springs, NC — the next major trail town — is roughly 30 miles beyond. Start planning your Hot Springs resupply and lodging from here.

Getting Supplies

NORTH — DAVENPORT GAP

Davenport Gap → Cosby, TN / Hot Springs, NC

4.9 trail miles north to Davenport Gap (TN-32/NC-284). From the gap, TN-32 heads northwest toward Cosby, TN (~5 road miles, limited services) and eventually Newport, TN (~20 miles, full grocery and services). Most thru-hikers push through Davenport Gap and continue hiking toward Hot Springs, NC (30 trail miles). Hot Springs is a full trail town with a hostel, inn, grocery, restaurants, and post office.

NEARBY — COSBY CAMPGROUND

GSMNP Cosby Campground

~2.5 miles on side trail north via Low Gap Trail. The Cosby Campground has a small camp store during the summer season with basic supplies. This is rarely used as a resupply by AT thru-hikers but serves as an emergency option if you're critically low on food. Hours and stock are unreliable — do not count on it as a primary resupply.

Where to Sleep

Cosby Knob Shelter (GSMNP Lean-to) Free (permit req.)
On trail · AT Mile ~222.4 · Permit required · Capacity 12
Chain-link enclosed lean-to above the Cosby Valley. Reliable spring water. Privy on site. Gate must stay latched. One of the quieter shelters in the park — the eastern Smokies see far less foot traffic than the Newfound Gap section. At 4,700 ft, temperatures are cooler but not as extreme as the high-elevation shelters. Reserve on your permit at recreation.gov.
Pros
  • Quiet, uncrowded section
  • Excellent wildlife habitat
  • Near park exit — finish line is close
  • Moderate elevation (relatively warmer)
Cons
  • Permit required and must be specific
  • No road access (4.9 mi to gap)
  • No cell service
  • Very active bear area
Hot Springs, NC — Hostel / Inn $25–120/night
~4.9 trail miles to Davenport Gap + ~30 trail miles to Hot Springs · Year-round
Hot Springs is one of the AT's great trail towns — a small village in the French Broad River valley where the AT walks directly through Main Street. Elmer's Sunnybank Inn and Hostel ($25–40 bunk, ~$70–100 private) is a legendary AT institution. The Hot Springs Resort and Spa offers mineral hot spring soaking. Full resupply, laundry, and post office in town. Plan your Davenport Gap exit to arrive in Hot Springs the following day.
Pros
  • The AT literally walks through town
  • Mineral hot springs (pay to soak)
  • Legendary hiker hostel (Elmer's)
  • Full resupply, post office
Cons
  • ~35 miles away (Davenport Gap + trail)
  • Small town — limited options
  • Elmer's fills fast in peak season
