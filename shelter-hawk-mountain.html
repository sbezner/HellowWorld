Shelter Overview

AT Mile: 8.1 from Springer
Elevation: ~3,200 ft
Capacity: ~8 hikers
Type: Lean-to
County: Gilmer, GA
Water: Spring 100 yds from shelter

Hawk Mountain Shelter sits on a ridge below Hawk Mountain's summit (~3,658 ft) in the southern portion of the Georgia AT. At mile 8.1, this is a natural second-night stop for NOBO hikers who camped at Stover Creek and are now midway through their first hundred miles. The trail climbs moderately through hardwood forest to reach the shelter, which is set back from the AT on a blue-blazed side trail.

The shelter is a standard lean-to with good tent sites in the surrounding area. A spring provides reliable water nearby. The ridge location means cool nights even in summer and potential for icy conditions in winter and early spring — come prepared for subfreezing temps in February and March.

Black bears frequent this area. Hang all food from the provided cables or use a bear canister. Never cook in or near the shelter.
Water: Spring approximately 100 yards east of the shelter. Reliable in all but the driest conditions. Treat before drinking.
Day 2 mileage note: Some hikers push from Springer to Hawk Mountain in one long day (8.1 miles). Others split it at Stover Creek (2.6 mi) then Hawk Mountain (5.5 more miles). Both are reasonable depending on fitness and pack weight.

Getting Supplies

At mile 8, the trail is still remote. The forest service roads in this area are rough and not suitable for standard vehicles. Your best path to supplies is continuing north to Neel Gap (23 more trail miles) or arranging a shuttle from Three Forks (~2.5 miles south on trail, accessible via USFS Road 58).

KEEP HIKING — 23.6 miles ahead

Mountain Crossings at Neel Gap — Mile 31.7

Continue north through Gooch Mountain (mile 15.8), over Ramrock Mountain, and through Woody Gap to reach Neel Gap. This is the standard first resupply. Pack at least 4 days of food from your starting point to comfortably cover Springer → Neel Gap without stressing about supplies.

BAIL-OUT POINT

Three Forks / USFS Road 58 — ~2.5 mi south

Three Forks is a trail junction near Springer with road access via Forest Service Road 58 (rough but passable in good conditions). From here you can reach Dahlonega (~18 miles by road) or call a shuttle. This is a gear/emergency bail-out point, not a supply destination.
