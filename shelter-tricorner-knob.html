Tricorner Knob Shelter

Great Smoky Mountains NP  ·  AT Mile ~216.7  ·  Lean-to  ·  Elev. ~5,920 ft

🏔 Great Smoky Mountains National Park — Permit Required Purchase at recreation.gov before entering the park. Cost: $40 + $8/person/night. Must stay at the shelter listed on your permit. Chain-link enclosure gate must stay latched. Call (865) 436-1231 to modify reservations.

Shelter Overview

AT Mile ~216.7
Elevation ~5,920 ft
Capacity 12
Type Lean-to (chain-link enclosure)
Water Spring 0.2 mi south on side trail
Privy Yes
Fee Free (permit required)

Tricorner Knob Shelter is named for the three-way ridge junction at Tricorner Knob just above — where the main Smokies ridge meets the Balsam Mountain ridge and another spur heading southeast into NC. At 5,920 feet, this is one of the highest shelters in the Smokies and the second-highest on the AT (after Mount Collins). The boreal spruce-fir forest here is among the most intact high-elevation ecosystems remaining in the eastern United States.

The ridge junction at Tricorner Knob was historically an important navigational waypoint and is still a landmark for backcountry hikers in the eastern Smokies. The views from the knob itself are outstanding — east into the Great Balsam Mountains, north into Tennessee, and south into the deep wilderness of the Smokies' eastern backcountry. The surrounding forest shows significant woolly adelgid damage to the Fraser firs, with ghostly silver snags standing above a recovering understory of fir seedlings.

A spring 0.2 miles south on a side trail provides reliable water — slightly further than at most shelters, so fill up at the previous water source if you're arriving late. Privy maintained on site. The eastern Smokies from here to Davenport Gap is the least-trafficked section of the traverse; you may have the shelter to yourself on weekdays outside of peak season.

High-Elevation Cold: At nearly 6,000 ft, Tricorner Knob is cold. Even in July, overnight temperatures regularly drop into the 40s. Hypothermia risk is real if you arrive wet from afternoon thunderstorms. Have your insulation layers accessible, not buried in your pack.

Getting Supplies

PUSH NORTH

Davenport Gap — 10.6 miles north

~10.6 trail miles north to Davenport Gap (TN-32 / NC-284). From Davenport Gap, you exit GSMNP and enter the Hot Springs section. The road at the gap provides access to Cosby, TN (limited services, ~5 road miles) or a longer hitch toward Newport, TN (~20 road miles, full grocery). Most NOBO thru-hikers push through Davenport Gap and continue toward Hot Springs, NC (another 30 miles) for the next major resupply.

SOUTH

Gatlinburg via Newfound Gap

~19.7 trail miles west to Newfound Gap + 15 road miles. A substantial backtrack at this point. Only worth considering if you have a genuine emergency or need to pick up a package from the Gatlinburg post office. If you're low on food at Tricorner Knob, push the 10.6 miles to Davenport Gap rather than backtracking 20 miles.

Where to Sleep

Tricorner Knob Shelter (GSMNP Lean-to) Free (permit req.)
On trail · AT Mile ~216.7 · Permit required · Capacity 12
High-elevation chain-link enclosed lean-to at a historic ridge junction. Spring water 0.2 mi south. Privy on site. Gate must stay latched. One of the quietest shelters on the traverse — the eastern Smokies see much less foot traffic than the western section. At 5,920 ft, bring serious insulation.
Pros
  • Remote, uncrowded eastern Smokies
  • Near Tricorner Knob viewpoint
  • Old-growth spruce-fir setting
  • Free (just permit fee)
Cons
  • Very cold at ~6,000 ft
  • Spring 0.2 mi away — fill up before arriving
  • No road access in either direction (10+ miles)
  • No cell service
