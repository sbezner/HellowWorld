Russell Field Shelter

Great Smoky Mountains National Park — NC/TN Border — AT Mile ~174.2
Great Smoky Mountains NP — Permit Required All AT shelters in GSMNP require a reservation through recreation.gov. Cost: $40 reservation fee + $8 per person per night. Purchase your permit before entering the park — you cannot buy one on the trail. All shelters have chain-link bear enclosures: you must sleep inside the enclosure and the gate must remain latched at all times. No dispersed camping is permitted anywhere in the park. You must stay at the shelter listed on your permit — to change shelters, call (865) 436-1231.

Russell Field Shelter sits near one of the first true open balds encountered northbound through the Smokies. The surrounding terrain opens up dramatically compared to the dense forest of the approach from Fontana Dam — hikers arriving here often get their first sweeping glimpse of the Tennessee side of the main ridge. The shelter is a standard Smokies structure with a chain-link bear enclosure and a privy on-site.

The nearby Russell Field bald is an open grassy area with sweeping views west into the Unicoi Mountains and south toward Fontana Lake. A 0.5-mile side trail leads to the bald summit and is one of the more rewarding short detours in this section of the park.

Side Trip — Russell Field Bald: Follow the 0.5-mile side trail from near the shelter to the bald summit. Views extend west into the Unicoi Mountains and south to Fontana Lake. This is one of the first true open balds northbound in the Smokies — a preview of the spectacular bald country ahead at Spence Field and Silers Bald.

GSMNP Rules at a Glance

Permit required before entering: Book at recreation.gov ($40 + $8/person/night). You cannot legally camp here without a valid permit for this specific shelter.
  • Sleep inside the chain-link bear enclosure — no exceptions.
  • Keep the enclosure gate latched at all times, even while entering or exiting.
  • No dispersed camping anywhere in Great Smoky Mountains NP.
  • Stay only at the shelter listed on your permit. To change shelters, call (865) 436-1231.
  • Campfires only in established fire rings where permitted — check current conditions for fire bans.
  • Pack out all trash; leave no trace standards apply throughout the park.

Getting Supplies

Russell Field is at mile ~174.2 — deep in the Smokies with no resupply inside the park. Plan your food carry carefully before entering at Fontana Dam.

Fontana Village, NC

Recommended — Southbound / Pre-Entry

The last full resupply before entering GSMNP from the south. Fontana Village has a camp store with hiker basics, a restaurant, and a marina store. From the Fontana Dam trailhead it is a short walk or hitch. Stock up here for the full Smokies traverse — plan at least 5–6 days of food to reach Gatlinburg without an in-park resupply.

Gatlinburg, TN

Alternate — Northbound Exit

The next major resupply north, accessible via a hitch from Newfound Gap (US-441, ~15 road miles west into Gatlinburg). From Russell Field, Newfound Gap is roughly 26+ trail miles away — plan 4+ days of food to cover that stretch. Gatlinburg has Food City, Walmart, and multiple outfitters with full resupply options.

No resupply inside the park. The Smokies represents one of the longest no-resupply stretches on the entire AT. Most northbound thru-hikers carry food from Fontana Village all the way to Gatlinburg — roughly 70+ trail miles total.

Where to Sleep Nearby

Russell Field Shelter
Permit Required
AT Mile ~174.2 • Capacity: 14 • Chain-link bear enclosure • Privy on-site • Spring 0.1 mi
The shelter is a classic Smokies lean-to with an enclosed chain-link bear pen. Sleeping inside the enclosure is mandatory under GSMNP regulations. Water is available from a spring 0.1 miles down a side trail. The site is well-positioned for hikers doing the standard shelter-to-shelter itinerary through the park and offers easy access to the Russell Field bald.

Fontana Village Resort
Paid • ~6 mi south
Fontana Dam, NC • ~6 trail miles south of Russell Field • Cabins, rooms, hostel bunks available
Fontana Village offers a full resort experience just outside the southern boundary of GSMNP. Amenities include cabins, a hotel, restaurant, camp store, laundry, and showers. It is the closest town-style lodging to Russell Field Shelter. Most northbound thru-hikers stop here before entering the park rather than backtracking after.
Pros
  • Full resort amenities

Cons

  • Resort-level pricing
  • Requires exiting the park
  • Loses northbound progress
