Davenport Gap Shelter

Great Smoky Mountains NP  ·  AT Mile ~227.3  ·  Lean-to  ·  Elev. ~1,975 ft

🏁 Final Shelter in the Great Smoky Mountains National Park Davenport Gap Shelter sits just inside the northeastern park boundary. Exiting the park here, you've completed the 59-mile GSMNP traverse from Fontana Dam — one of the greatest wilderness walks in eastern North America. The Hot Springs, NC section begins 0.1 miles north at the park boundary sign.
🏔 Great Smoky Mountains National Park — Last Night Under Permit Your GSMNP permit covers your stay at Davenport Gap Shelter. This is your final required permit night. North of the park boundary, GSMNP regulations no longer apply. Purchase permit at recreation.gov: $40 + $8/person/night. Call (865) 436-1231 for permit modifications.

Davenport Gap Shelter is the final shelter inside Great Smoky Mountains National Park for NOBO thru-hikers, positioned near the park's northeastern boundary where the AT descends from the main Smokies ridge to cross TN-32/NC-284 at Davenport Gap. At 1,975 feet, this is the lowest of the Smokies AT shelters — a dramatic elevation drop from the 5,000+ foot heights traversed over the preceding days.

The descent from Cosby Knob to Davenport Gap loses nearly 2,700 feet over 4.9 miles — steep, rocky, and demanding on the knees. The forest transitions from high-elevation spruce-fir through mixed hardwoods to the lower cove hardwood community typical of the Smokies' lower elevations. By the time you reach Davenport Gap, you're back in familiar southern Appalachian territory: oak, hickory, tulip poplar, and the ubiquitous rhododendron thickets.

The road at Davenport Gap — TN-32 on the Tennessee side and NC-284 on the North Carolina side — provides the trail's first reliable road access since Newfound Gap, 20 miles back. Hitchhiking is possible from the gap in either direction, though traffic is light on this rural mountain road. Most thru-hikers continue north toward Hot Springs, NC rather than diverting to Cosby or Newport.

Park Exit: The official GSMNP boundary is 0.1 miles north of the shelter at a signed park boundary marker. Once you cross it, you are no longer subject to GSMNP regulations — dispersed camping becomes legal again per USFS rules on the Cherokee National Forest land ahead.

Milestone: You've Crossed the Smokies

From Fontana Dam (AT Mile 164.7) to Davenport Gap (AT Mile 227.3) is 62.6 miles through the Great Smoky Mountains — the most celebrated section of the southern AT. You've crossed the highest point on the entire trail (Clingmans Dome, 6,643 ft), walked past some of the last old-growth forest in the eastern US, and traversed the most-visited national park in America entirely on foot.

Behind you: 227+ miles of Appalachian Trail since Springer Mountain in Georgia. Ahead: Hot Springs, NC (30 miles), Roan Highlands (100+ miles), Damascus, VA, the Shenandoah, and eventually Katahdin. You are through the hardest and most remote sections of the southern AT. The trail continues to be challenging, but the deep wilderness is behind you.

Getting Supplies

RECOMMENDED

Hot Springs, NC — 30 trail miles north

~30 trail miles north via Max Patch and Walnut Mountain areas. Hot Springs is one of the AT's most beloved trail towns. The trail literally walks through the middle of downtown. Elmer's Sunnybank Inn and Hostel is a legendary AT institution. Full grocery, laundry, post office, restaurants. The French Broad River runs through town and there's a mineral hot springs spa. Plan 2 days from Davenport Gap to Hot Springs. Mail drop: Elmer's Inn, 26 Serpentine Ave, Hot Springs, NC 28743.

ROAD ACCESS

Cosby, TN / Newport, TN

From Davenport Gap: TN-32 north ~5 miles to Cosby (limited), ~20 miles to Newport (full grocery). Newport, TN has a Walmart and full services but requires a significant road hitch. Mostly used as an emergency exit rather than a routine resupply. Most thru-hikers prefer continuing on trail to Hot Springs rather than diverting to Newport.

Where to Sleep

Davenport Gap Shelter (GSMNP Lean-to) Free (permit req.)
On trail · AT Mile ~227.3 · Last GSMNP shelter · Capacity 12
Final Smokies shelter in the chain-link enclosure system. Low elevation (1,975 ft) means warmer nights than the high Smokies. Spring water on side trail. Privy on site. Gate must stay latched — you're still in GSMNP until 0.1 miles north. This shelter often sees celebrating thru-hikers on their last park night. The emotional weight of completing the Smokies traverse makes this a memorable camp.
Pros
  • Final milestone shelter — Smokies complete
  • Low elevation — warmest Smokies shelter
  • Near road access at Davenport Gap
  • Free (just permit fee)
Cons
  • Permit still required (last night)
  • Light road traffic at gap for hitchhiking
  • No cell service on approach
Elmer's Sunnybank Inn & Hostel — Hot Springs, NC ~$25–40 bunk / $70–100 private
~30 trail miles north · Year-round (call ahead)
Elmer's is legendary — a Victorian farmhouse in the heart of Hot Springs that has been welcoming AT thru-hikers for decades. Bunk beds, private rooms, family-style vegetarian dinners, and a genuine community atmosphere. One of the AT's most beloved stopovers. Reserve ahead in peak season: 26 Serpentine Ave, Hot Springs, NC 28743. The French Broad River is steps away, and the mineral hot springs spa is a short walk.
Pros
  • Legendary AT hiker culture destination
  • Affordable bunk rates
  • Family-style dinners included (some nights)
  • Mineral hot springs spa nearby
  • AT walks through downtown Hot Springs
Cons
  • 30 trail miles away — two days of hiking
  • Must call ahead — fills in spring season
  • Small town with limited backup options
