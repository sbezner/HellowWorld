Carter Gap Shelter is a classic Nantahala Hiking Club lean-to sitting in the gap between Albert Mountain and Wayah Bald country. At 4,540 feet, the shelter is sheltered from the prevailing winds by the ridgeline above, making it a more comfortable overnight than some of the exposed high-elevation campsites nearby. A signed spring 0.1 miles down a side trail provides reliable water year-round.

Many thru-hikers use Carter Gap as a strategic overnight, positioning themselves for the final push to Winding Stair Gap and Franklin the following day. The shelter is approximately 10 trail miles from Winding Stair Gap, making it an ideal camp-then-resupply staging point. The lean-to sleeps 8 with tent pads for overflow, and the privy is maintained in good condition by the NHC.

Albert Mountain's fire tower, accessible via a 0.5-mile rocky scramble just south of here, is one of the AT's more dramatic viewpoints in North Carolina — 360-degree views from an old fire lookout tower on the rocky summit. Worth the side trip on a clear day, especially since the climb happens before you reach Carter Gap heading northbound.

Albert Mountain Fire Tower: Located about 1 mile south of Carter Gap on the AT. The rocky summit scramble is short but requires hands-on climbing in spots. The views from the top are some of the best in the southern AT's NC section.

Getting Supplies

RECOMMENDED

Franklin, NC via Winding Stair Gap (US-64)

~10 trail miles north, then ~10 road miles west. Continue north through Big Spring Gap and Rock Gap to Winding Stair Gap on US-64. Hitchhike west ~10 miles to Franklin. This is the standard resupply for hikers in the Carter Gap area. Budget Inn offers a free shuttle from the gap if you call ahead. Ingles Supermarket and Outdoor 76 are both in downtown Franklin.

ALTERNATE

Wallace Gap (Old US-64)

~4 trail miles north. An older road crossing at Wallace Gap — remote, light traffic, hard to hitch from. No services at the gap itself. Only practical if you have a pre-arranged pickup. Far inferior to waiting for Winding Stair Gap another 6 miles ahead.

Mail Drop: Outdoor 76, 74 E Main St, Franklin, NC 28734. Holds packages for thru-hikers at no charge. Call ahead: (828) 524-8801.

On trail · AT Mile ~91.7 · Open year-round · Capacity 8 + tenting
Well-maintained lean-to in a protected gap. Spring water nearby, privy on site. Bear cables present — use them. Tent pads handle overflow. At 4,540 ft elevation, nights are cool to cold even in July. The shelter is often used by section hikers from the Standing Indian Campground area, so it can fill on spring and fall weekends.
