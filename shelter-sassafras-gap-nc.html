Sassafras Gap Shelter

Elevation ~3,440 ft

Type Lean-to

Sassafras Gap Shelter occupies a forested gap at moderate elevation in the Cheoah Mountains — the rugged range between the Nantahala Gorge and Fontana Lake. This section of the AT, from the NOC through the Cheeohs to Fontana Dam, is one of the more demanding stretches in the southern AT: steep, relentless climbs and descents with no significant road access for 30+ miles. The Cheoahs have a well-earned reputation for being tough terrain even for experienced hikers.

The shelter itself is a standard NHC lean-to with 8-person capacity, a privy, and a spring on a blue-blaze side trail 0.15 miles from the main AT. The spring is reliable in most conditions but should be filtered. The surrounding forest is mature second-growth hardwood with significant sassafras — the shelter's namesake — scattered throughout the understory. Sassafras in fall displays brilliant orange and red foliage even when surrounding trees are still green.

Sassafras Gap is roughly 4.5 miles north of the NOC — making it a common first-night stop after leaving the NOC campus. The proximity to the NOC means some hikers use it to "break camp" before diving into the Cheoahs proper. From here, it's approximately 26 more miles to Fontana Dam through increasingly wild terrain.

The Cheoah Mountains: This section (NOC to Fontana) is legendary for its difficulty — steeper elevation changes per mile than almost anywhere on the southern AT. The Cheeohs are not to be underestimated. Carry more water than you think you need; springs are spaced further apart here than in the Nantahala section.

Getting Supplies

SOUTHBOUND (RESUPPLY)

Nantahala Outdoor Center — 4.5 miles south

4.5 trail miles south on AT. The NOC is a 2–3 hour hike south. It has the Wesser Store (outfitter + food), River's End Restaurant, and dorm lodging. If you didn't fully resupply at the NOC before leaving, returning is your only practical option from Sassafras Gap. See the NOC page for details. Mail drop: 13077 US-19 W, Bryson City, NC 28713.

NORTHBOUND

Fontana Village

~26 trail miles north. Fontana Village (300 Woods Rd, Fontana Dam, NC 28733) is the next resupply north. It has a small camp store, marina store, and restaurant. Stock is limited compared to the NOC — it's primarily a resort village. Plan food for 2–3 days from Sassafras Gap to Fontana Village, then resupply for the Smokies at the NOC or Fontana.

Critical Planning: The stretch from the NOC to Fontana Dam (30+ miles) has essentially no road access. Load up at the NOC with 3 full days of food before starting this section. There is no bail-out option from Sassafras Gap.
