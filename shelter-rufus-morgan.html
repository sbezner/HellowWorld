A. Rufus Morgan Shelter

North Carolina  ·  AT Mile ~134.0  ·  Lean-to  ·  Elev. ~1,700 ft

Gateway to the Nantahala Outdoor Center This shelter sits 4.5 miles north of the NOC at Wesser, NC — close enough to smell the River's End Restaurant cooking. A. Rufus Morgan Shelter is named for the Reverend Alvin Rufus Morgan (1885–1983), one of the most beloved figures in the history of the Appalachian Trail. Morgan was a founding member and longtime leader of the Nantahala Hiking Club, which maintains this section of the AT. He reportedly hiked sections of the trail well into his 90s and was deeply committed to both the trail and the Appalachian mountain community. Spending the night at his namesake shelter carries a certain historical weight.

The shelter itself sits at a relatively low elevation of ~1,700 feet — a dramatic change from the high balds and ridges traversed since Franklin. The descent from Wayah Bald through the Wesser Creek drainage is one of the more significant elevation drops on the southern AT: you'll lose nearly 3,500 feet over roughly 19 miles. The low elevation means warmer temperatures, more hardwood canopy, and a noticeably different ecosystem from the spruce and beech forests above.

A reliable creek flows near the shelter and provides consistent water. The surrounding forest is dense second-growth mixed hardwood with rhododendron thickets — classic Nantahala Gorge terrain. The proximity to the NOC (4.5 miles north) means this shelter is sometimes used as a last-night stop before arriving at the NOC, or a first-night stop after leaving it heading south.

Rev. Morgan's Legacy: A. Rufus Morgan was ordained as an Episcopal priest in 1910 and served communities across western NC for 73 years while simultaneously advocating for and maintaining the AT. He is an extraordinary figure in the AT's history. The plaque at this shelter is worth reading.

Getting Supplies

RECOMMENDED

Nantahala Outdoor Center (NOC) — 4.5 miles north

4.5 trail miles north on AT. The NOC at Wesser is an easy half-day hike north. The campus includes the Wesser Store (outfitter + food), River's End Restaurant (hot meals, beer), and full lodging. Mail drops accepted at: 13077 US-19 W, Bryson City, NC 28713. This is the obvious resupply for anyone at Rufus Morgan. See the NOC page for complete details on services, hours, and lodging.

ALTERNATE

Bryson City, NC (via NOC shuttle)

Via NOC shuttle or hitch from NOC, ~9 road miles east. The larger town of Bryson City offers a full grocery store, additional restaurants, and chain motels. Typically accessed via shuttle from the NOC rather than directly. The NOC can sometimes arrange or recommend shuttles to Bryson City. Better option for major resupply if the Wesser Store's selection is insufficient.

NOC Mail Drop: Nantahala Outdoor Center, 13077 US-19 W, Bryson City, NC 28713. Address to yourself c/o NOC Outfitter. Packages must arrive within 2 weeks of your pickup — call ahead to confirm current policies.

Where to Sleep Nearby

A. Rufus Morgan Shelter (AT Lean-to) Free
On trail · AT Mile ~134.0 · Open year-round · Capacity 6 + tenting
Historic lean-to at low elevation in the Nantahala Gorge drainage. Reliable water nearby. Privy on site. Bear cables present. At 1,700 ft, this shelter is significantly warmer than the high-elevation shelters to the south — a welcome change in cold weather. Tent pads nearby for overflow. The shelter honors one of the AT's most important historical figures.
Pros
  • Historically significant site
  • Low elevation — warmer nights
  • Close to NOC (4.5 mi)
  • Free, no reservation
Cons
  • Small capacity (6)
  • No cell service
  • Rhododendron thickets — feels enclosed
  • Mice in shelter
NOC Basecamp Dorm — Wesser, NC ~$35–45/bunk
4.5 trail miles north · Year-round
The NOC's bunk-style dorm lodging is the budget choice on the NOC campus. Shared bathrooms, hot showers, coin laundry. Bring your sleeping bag or rent bedding on-site. The dorm atmosphere is communal and hiker-friendly — a natural gathering point for AT thru-hikers. River's End Restaurant is steps away. See the NOC page for all details.
Pros
  • Affordable bunk rate
  • Hot showers and laundry on campus
  • Restaurant steps away
  • Community of hikers
Cons
  • 4.5 miles away
  • Shared facilities
  • Can be crowded on weekends
NOC Lodge / Cabins — Wesser, NC ~$90–150/night
