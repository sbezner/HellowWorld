Nantahala Outdoor Center (NOC)

Appalachian Trail, North Carolina — AT Mile ~137 (NOBO) • Nantahala River Gorge • The Mountain Crossings of North Carolina

The NOC is right on the Appalachian Trail at the Nantahala River crossing. Restaurant, outfitter, gear store, lodging, laundry, and showers — all without leaving the trail corridor. One of the most beloved stops on the entire southern AT.

What Is the NOC?

AT Mile: ~137 (NOBO)
Elevation: ~1,700 ft (river gorge)
River: Nantahala River
County: Swain County, NC
Nearest Town: Bryson City, ~12 mi

The Nantahala Outdoor Center is a full-service adventure resort built into a dramatic river gorge in the Nantahala Mountains of western North Carolina. The AT descends from the ridgeline to cross the Nantahala River right through the heart of the NOC campus — you literally walk past the restaurant, gear store, and lodging on your way from the southbound A. Rufus Morgan Shelter to the northbound climb.

Founded in 1972, the NOC has grown into one of the premier outdoor adventure centers in the Southeast. For AT hikers, it functions as a mini-town: you can resupply, get a hot meal, do laundry, charge your devices, repair gear, and get a real bed — all in one place, without a road shuttle. During NOBO season (March–May), the River's End Restaurant fills daily with thru-hikers fueling up on burgers and breakfast platters before the long climb out of the gorge.

The gorge setting is spectacular. The Nantahala River runs cold and clear — it's dam-released water from Nantahala Lake and stays around 45°F year-round, making it one of the premier whitewater rafting destinations in the East. Expect to see kayakers, rafters, and adventure tourists alongside AT hikers, especially on summer weekends.

Closest AT shelter: A. Rufus Morgan Shelter is ~0.5 miles south on the AT (just before the NOC northbound). Named for a legendary AT trail maintainer who worked on the trail into his 90s.

River's End Restaurant

The most popular restaurant on the southern AT. Serves breakfast, lunch, and dinner with a hiker-friendly menu — enormous portions of burgers, sandwiches, wraps, breakfast burritos, and daily specials. The "Hiker Hunger" is real and River's End delivers. Hours vary by season; typically open 7 AM–9 PM during peak AT season. Cash and cards accepted.

Wesser Store (Resupply)

The Wesser Store stocks hiker food (Clif bars, ramen, instant oatmeal, Knorr sides, candy), fuel canisters, basic first aid, and limited gear items. Selection is sufficient for a resupply if you're tight on food but not as comprehensive as a full outfitter like Mountain Crossings. Mail drops accepted — send to: Wesser Store / NOC, 13077 US-19 W, Bryson City NC 28713. Include your name and ETA.

NOC Outfitter

Full gear shop with hiking equipment, clothing, maps, water filters, trekking poles, footwear, and more. Staff are experienced outdoor professionals. Good for gear repairs and replacements — especially footwear, which takes a beating by mile 134.

~$35–45/night
Nantahala Outdoor Center, 13077 US-19 W, Bryson City NC 28713 — right on the AT
The hiker-oriented dormitory lodging at the NOC. Bunk beds in shared rooms, hot showers, laundry access, and proximity to River's End Restaurant. The go-to option for thru-hikers who want a real bed without leaving the trail campus. Fills up fast on weekends and during peak NOBO season — call ahead or book online.

Hotels in Bryson City, NC
~$80–160/night
Bryson City, NC — ~12 miles from NOC by road on US-19
Bryson City is the nearest full-service town to the NOC. Several hotels and B&Bs including Everett Hotel (boutique, downtown, ~$120-160), various chain motels, and hiker-friendly options. Getting to Town from the NOC

NOC Shuttle Service
PAID — varies
The NOC runs shuttles to Bryson City and surrounding areas. Rates and schedules vary by season. Call the NOC front desk to arrange — they also do longer shuttles to Fontana Dam, Gatlinburg, and other AT access points. Best source for current shuttle info.
Call NOC: 888-662-1662 noc.com
Bryson City Outdoors / Local Shuttles
PAID
Multiple local shuttle operators serve the NOC–Bryson City corridor. Ask at the NOC outfitter for the current recommended driver list — they keep a live update of who's operating. Hitchhiking US-19 from the NOC is possible but the road is winding and traffic is moderate.
Hitchhiking from NOC: US-19 runs along the Nantahala River and is a two-lane mountain highway. Traffic is moderate — rafting tourists and locals. Stand at the NOC entrance on US-19. West goes to Bryson City (~12 miles); east goes back toward Franklin (~25 miles). Average wait: 20–40 minutes. Weekends are much easier.
