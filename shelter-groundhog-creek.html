Groundhog Creek Shelter

North Carolina  ·  AT Mile ~249.0  ·  Lean-to  ·  Elev. ~2,850 ft

First Shelter North of the Smokies: Groundhog Creek is the first shelter after leaving Great Smoky Mountains National Park. GSMNP regulations no longer apply — dispersed camping is legal again on Cherokee National Forest and Pisgah National Forest land. Standing Bear Farm hostel is about 20 miles south near I-40 if you need to resupply before reaching this shelter.

Groundhog Creek Shelter sits at approximately 2,850 feet in a cove hardwood forest along the Pisgah National Forest section of the Appalachian Trail. Located 0.2 miles east of the AT at Deep Gap, this lean-to sleeps 10 on a refurbished sleeping platform (rebuilt 2023) and is maintained by the Carolina Mountain Club.

The shelter area includes 8 tent sites west of the trail at Deep Gap, making it a good option even when the lean-to fills up. A bear box was installed in February 2023, and bear cables are also available — use them. Bear activity is common throughout the Davenport Gap to Hot Springs corridor. The stream runs about 100 yards from the shelter and flows reliably except in extreme drought. Always filter or treat.

This section of trail between Standing Bear Farm (near I-40) and Max Patch climbs through remote forest with limited bail-out options. The terrain is moderate compared to the Smokies but still involves significant elevation changes. Cell service is unreliable to nonexistent in this area. Plan your water and food carries accordingly.

Max Patch Ahead: Max Patch (4,629 ft) is approximately 5 miles north — one of the most spectacular bald summits on the entire AT. The 360-degree views of the surrounding mountains make it a highlight of the Davenport Gap to Hot Springs section. Many hikers camp on or near the bald for sunset and sunrise, though USFS regulations require camping at designated sites.

Getting Supplies

Groundhog Creek is between two resupply options: Standing Bear Farm behind you and Hot Springs ahead. Most thru-hikers carry enough food from Standing Bear to reach Hot Springs without stopping.

RECOMMENDED

Hot Springs, NC — ~25 trail miles north

~25 trail miles north via Max Patch and Walnut Mountain. Hot Springs is one of the AT's most beloved trail towns — the trail literally walks through downtown. Full grocery, laundry, post office, restaurants, outfitter, and the famous mineral hot springs spa. Elmer's Sunnybank Inn is a legendary AT hostel. Plan 1.5–2 days from Groundhog Creek. Mail drop: Elmer's Inn, 26 Serpentine Ave, Hot Springs, NC 28743.

BEHIND YOU

Standing Bear Farm — ~20 trail miles south

~20 trail miles south near I-40/Pigeon River. Standing Bear Farm is a rustic hostel and resupply point at Green Corner Road, 0.2 miles off the AT. Limited resupply (ramen, snacks, freeze-dried meals) but enough to supplement a food carry. Bunks, camping, laundry, and hot showers available. Text Maria at (423) 608-0149 for info. Open year-round 8am–8pm.
