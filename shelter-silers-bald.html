Silers Bald Shelter

Great Smoky Mountains National Park — NC/TN Border — AT Mile ~192.0
Great Smoky Mountains NP — Permit Required All AT shelters in GSMNP require a reservation through recreation.gov. Cost: $40 reservation fee + $8 per person per night. Purchase your permit before entering the park — you cannot buy one on the trail. All shelters have chain-link bear enclosures: you must sleep inside the enclosure and the gate must remain latched at all times. No dispersed camping is permitted anywhere in the park. You must stay at the shelter listed on your permit — to change shelters, call (865) 436-1231.

Silers Bald Shelter is one of the most celebrated camp spots in the Great Smoky Mountains, positioned just below one of the finest open balds in the park. Silers Bald itself (5,607 ft) sits 0.15 miles north of the shelter on the AT and features a large open summit meadow with 360-degree views — on a clear day encompassing Mount LeConte, Clingmans Dome, and the full sweep of the Smokies main ridge in both directions.

Sunsets from Silers Bald are frequently described as among the most extraordinary on the entire Appalachian Trail. The combination of the high open summit, proximity to Clingmans Dome (the AT high point at 6,643 ft), and the unobstructed western horizon creates exceptional light. The shelter itself sits in light forest cover just below the bald, offering some wind protection.

Name confusion: Do not confuse Silers Bald (inside GSMNP, ~5,607 ft) with Siler Bald in NC south of Franklin (~5,216 ft). They are different mountains on different sections of the AT, roughly 90 trail miles apart. This page covers the Smokies version — higher, more dramatic, and with much more extensive views.
Bald summit access: The AT passes directly over Silers Bald summit, 0.15 miles north of the shelter. Walk up before dinner for sunset or before dawn for sunrise — both are extraordinary at this elevation with unobstructed views.

GSMNP Rules at a Glance

Permit required before entering: Book at recreation.gov ($40 + $8/person/night). You cannot legally camp here without a valid permit for this specific shelter.
  • Sleep inside the chain-link bear enclosure — no exceptions.
  • Keep the enclosure gate latched at all times, even while entering or exiting.
  • No dispersed camping anywhere in Great Smoky Mountains NP.
  • Stay only at the shelter listed on your permit. To change shelters, call (865) 436-1231.
  • Campfires only in established fire rings where permitted — check current conditions for fire bans.
  • The bald meadow vegetation is fragile — stay on the AT and designated paths across the summit.

Getting Supplies

Silers Bald is at mile ~192.0, approximately 8 trail miles from Newfound Gap and a Gatlinburg hitch northbound. You are in the final stretch of the Smokies traverse — ensure you have enough food to reach Newfound Gap.

Gatlinburg, TN

Recommended — Northbound Exit

Gatlinburg is now the closest resupply — roughly 8 trail miles to Newfound Gap, then a hitch on US-441 (~15 road miles west into town). Gatlinburg has full grocery (Food City, Walmart), gear outfitters, and lodging. Budget option: Hostel in the Hollow (~$30/night bunks). Mid-range: various motels at $80–150/night. Splurge: LeConte Lodge (reservation required, expensive).

Fontana Village, NC

Alternate — Southbound / Pre-Entry

Fontana Village is roughly 23 trail miles south — not practical to return mid-traverse. If you are southbound or started from Newfound Gap, Fontana Village is where you will exit the park. Either way, carry sufficient food from your last town stop before entering.

Nearly through the Smokies: From Silers Bald, you are about 8 miles from Newfound Gap and Clingmans Dome. Most hikers push hard through this stretch to reach Gatlinburg for resupply. If you are running low on food, this is manageable — but do not linger unnecessarily.
